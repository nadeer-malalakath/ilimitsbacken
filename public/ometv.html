📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear look at how OmeTV works and why its design creates specific risks for younger users.

OmeTV connects users to completely random strangers through live video chat — instantly, with no prior relationship, screening or context. Users can be matched with anyone in the world, at any time, with no way of knowing who they will encounter next.
🎲
Instant Random Matching
Every session connects the user live with a completely unknown person. There is no friend list, no mutual connection and no shared context — just a random live video feed.
🌍
Global, Unfiltered Reach
Matches can come from anywhere in the world. Children may be connected with adults of any background, intent or disposition with no geographic or demographic filtering.
🪪
No Identity Verification
The platform does not verify a user's age, identity or location. Anyone — including adults with harmful intentions — can join and begin matching immediately.
⏭️
One-Tap Skip Design
Users skip through strangers in rapid succession. The fast-paced, game-like format is designed to maximise time on the platform and can make unsafe interactions feel ordinary.
💬
Text Chat Runs Alongside Video
A live text chat panel accompanies every video connection, enabling strangers to share links, personal information requests or inappropriate messages in real time.
⚠️ Section 2

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Specific dangers that arise when children use anonymous live video platforms.

👤
Unknown Adults
Children can be instantly connected with unverified adults — with no moderation or pre-screening
🎭
Inappropriate Behaviour
Users have full latitude to display, say or share anything before a child can skip or report them
🚫
No Age Verification
Anyone of any age can sign up — children and predatory adults coexist on the same platform
🪤
Grooming Risk
Anonymous chat is a known tool for building inappropriate trust with children over time
📸
Recording Without Consent
Third parties can silently record video sessions — children may not realise they are being captured on film
🔗
Link to Other Platforms
Strangers routinely share links directing children to external sites with unsuitable or harmful content
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Curiosity and Boredom Are the Main Drivers
Children are often drawn to random chat apps out of genuine social curiosity or simply looking for entertainment. The novelty of meeting new people online can feel exciting and harmless — without awareness of the risks involved.
2
Unpredictable Interactions Are the Feature, Not the Bug
The entire appeal of OmeTV is randomness. This same unpredictability is precisely what creates danger. There is no way for the platform — or the child — to know who will appear next or what that person intends.
3
Harmful Encounters Can Happen in Seconds
Unlike social media where exposure is gradual, live video delivers a harmful interaction the instant a match begins. Children can be exposed to adult content or inappropriate behaviour before they have any chance to react.
4
Guide Children Toward Safe Social Alternatives
The underlying need — to socialise, be seen and make connections — is entirely healthy. The goal isn't to shut down the impulse but to channel it toward platforms where identities are known, communities are moderated and interactions are age-appropriate.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to keep your child safer online.

💬
Encourage Communication Only With Known People
Help children understand the clear distinction between online strangers and people they know in real life. Explain that even a friendly-seeming stranger online is still unknown — and that no one online is owed personal information, photos or video access.
🔐
Discuss Online Privacy and Safety
Teach children never to share their name, school, location or any images with strangers online. Explain that video calls can be recorded without their knowledge — and that once something is shared, it cannot be taken back.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Prevent Unsafe Access
Technology-based safeguards are most effective when applied consistently across every device your child uses. Pair iLimits restrictions with regular, open conversations — controls set the boundary, but trust and dialogue are what make it last.
