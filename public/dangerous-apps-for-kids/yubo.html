What This App Does

A clear picture of Yubo — a social discovery and live streaming platform that encourages users to meet strangers, join group video sessions and interact with people from different locations they have never met before.

⚠️
"Youth-Friendly" Marketing Does Not Make Yubo Safe for Children
Yubo actively markets itself as a platform for young people and positions its social discovery features as age-appropriate. However, the same core mechanics that make it appealing — live video with new people, swipe-based matching and open group chats — carry the same underlying risks as other stranger-contact platforms, regardless of the audience it claims to target. Youth-focused branding is not a safety feature.

Global Discovery
Yubo is a social discovery and live streaming platform that allows users to meet new people, join live video streams and chat with others online. The app focuses on helping users discover and interact with people they do not already know through live streaming, group chats and social matching. Users can communicate with strangers from different locations and participate in live video sessions — with no meaningful verification of the ages or identities of the people they encounter.
👆
Swipe-Based Social Matching
Like dating apps, Yubo uses swipe mechanics for users to express interest in meeting strangers. Mutual interest unlocks direct messaging and video interaction — giving the discovery process a familiar, game-like quality that can lower a child's awareness of who they are actually agreeing to connect with.
📺
Open Live Video Streaming
Users can join or broadcast live video streams that are open to other Yubo users. Children participating in or watching these streams can encounter unpredictable content, adult behaviour or inappropriate conversations in real time — with no reliable content filtering and no advance indication of what a stream may contain before they have already joined it.
💬
Group Chats With Unknown Users
Yubo hosts group chat sessions where multiple unknown users can interact simultaneously. These group environments can expose children to a wider range of unknown adults at once, with less individual accountability for what is said and reduced ability to identify or report a specific person for inappropriate behaviour.
📩
Direct Messaging With Matched Strangers
Matched users can exchange private messages with no parental visibility or content moderation. Conversations between a child and an unknown adult are entirely private — and, as with other social discovery platforms, can develop into persistent contact well beyond the initial match.
🔓
Insufficient Age Verification
Although Yubo uses age-based onboarding and some moderation, its age verification is self-reported and easily bypassed. Adults can present as younger users, and children can access the platform without meaningful age-appropriate filtering — meaning the youth-friendly marketing does not translate into genuine youth-safe safeguards.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Social Discovery Platforms Are Built to Encourage Stranger Interaction
Unlike social media platforms where users communicate primarily with known contacts, Yubo's entire design is oriented towards helping users meet people they have never encountered before. Swipe matching, open live streams and group chats are all features designed specifically to facilitate new contact with strangers — and the risks that come with that contact exist regardless of how youth-oriented the platform's marketing is.
2
Youth-Friendly Branding Is Not a Reliable Safety Guarantee
Yubo positions itself as a platform for young people and invests significantly in that marketing identity. However, self-reported age at registration is the only meaningful gate — and it is trivially bypassed. An adult who wants to interact with younger users on Yubo faces no verification barrier. Parents should not assume that a platform marketed at teenagers is safe for their child by virtue of its target audience alone.
3
Live Streaming Creates Unpredictable, Real-Time Exposure
The live streaming component of Yubo shares the same fundamental risk as dedicated random video chat apps — children can be exposed to inappropriate content or adult behaviour in real time, with no opportunity to preview what a stream contains before they have already joined it. Because streams involve multiple users simultaneously, the social environment can shift quickly and in ways that are difficult for a child to navigate without adult guidance.
4
Children May See Yubo as a Normal Social App — Not a Risk
Because Yubo is actively marketed at young people and uses familiar social media mechanics — swiping, following, live content — children may perceive it as broadly equivalent to other platforms they use without concern. This normalisation of stranger-contact features within a youth-branded interface means children may not apply the caution they would to a more obviously adult platform, and may not disclose their use of the app to parents who would otherwise have concerns.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around safe online interactions and responsible social networking.

💬
Talk With Children About Safe Online Interactions
Have an open, ongoing conversation with your child about what safe online interaction looks like — and why platforms designed for meeting strangers carry different risks from apps used to stay in touch with known friends. Explain specifically that a platform being marketed at young people does not mean every person they encounter on it is also a young person, and that swipe-based matching and live streaming can connect them with adults who have deliberately bypassed age restrictions. Make it clear that you are approachable about what they encounter online — children who feel comfortable disclosing are significantly better protected than those who stay silent.
🤝
Encourage Responsible Use of Social Networking Platforms
Talk with your child about what responsible social networking looks like in practice — limiting contact to known people, not sharing personal information with strangers, recognising when a conversation is becoming uncomfortable and knowing how to disengage. If your child is drawn to Yubo's social features, explore safer alternatives that meet the same social needs within a more controlled environment — platforms where users interact with known contacts or within verified, moderated communities rather than through open stranger matching.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to Social Discovery Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Social discovery apps like Yubo are often reinstalled quickly after deletion, and may not be recognised by name by parents who are monitoring manually. Device-level category restrictions are significantly more reliable than app-by-app removal. Combine technical controls with ongoing, honest conversations about online safety to build the most complete and durable protection available for your family.
