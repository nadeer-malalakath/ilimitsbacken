Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to address advanced app cloning on your child's device and build lasting habits around honest, transparent online behaviour.

💬
Encourage Open Discussions About Responsible Online Behaviour
If you find Parallel Space Pro on your child's device, begin with an honest and non-confrontational conversation. Ask what was being cloned, which accounts were being run in the hidden environment, and — most importantly — why they felt a separate, concealed online presence was something they needed. Children who choose the Pro edition have often put deliberate thought into maintaining privacy, which makes the conversation more nuanced. Understanding the motivation behind a hidden identity — whether it is social exploration, peer pressure or something more concerning — allows you to respond proportionately and helpfully.
🧭
Explain the Risks of Maintaining Hidden or Secondary Accounts
Help your child understand that the risks of a hidden secondary account are not just about parental oversight — they are risks to the child themselves. A secondary account operating outside a child's known social environment tends to involve less caution: different standards around who to trust, what personal information to share, and how to respond to people they do not know. Without the social anchors of their primary account — friends, peers, shared context — children in hidden account environments are more exposed to manipulation, and less likely to apply the protective judgement they would use in a visible setting.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to App Cloning Tools
Apply iLimits restrictions across every device your child uses — including any secondary or older phones. The Pro edition's advanced concealment features make discovery-based approaches particularly unreliable — restriction before installation is the only approach that is consistently effective. Pair device-level blocking with regular open conversations about which accounts your child uses so that oversight covers both the technical and the relational dimensions of their safety.
