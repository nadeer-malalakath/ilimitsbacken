What This App Does

A clear picture of Fantasy Power 11 — a fantasy sports platform where knowledge of real cricket and football players is used to enter cash contests whose outcomes are determined by live match performance, combining the familiarity of sports fandom with the financial mechanics of a wagering platform.

🏆
Fantasy Power 11 Uses Sports Knowledge as the Entry Point Into Real-Money Competitive Contests
Fantasy Power 11 is built around an activity that feels natural to sports fans — picking players, following match performance and comparing results with other enthusiasts. Beneath this familiar framing is a real-money contest structure where entry fees are paid, outcomes are uncertain and cash prizes are distributed to top performers. For younger users who associate the app with cricket or football rather than financial risk, this framing makes it significantly harder to recognise that participating in a paid contest is a genuine monetary transaction with real stakes attached to the outcome.

Fantasy Power 11 is a fantasy sports and real-money gaming platform focused primarily on cricket and football. Users build virtual teams by selecting real-world players within a salary cap, then enter those teams into contests where points are earned based on how selected players actually perform during live matches. The platform operates paid contest tiers where a real-money entry fee is required to compete for cash prizes — with prize distribution determined by how a user's team ranks against all other paid entries in that contest.
🏏
Virtual Team Selection Driving Real-Money Contest Entry
Fantasy Power 11 asks users to build virtual cricket or football teams from real player rosters — a task that feels like an extension of sports fandom and requires genuine knowledge of the game. Once a team is built, it is entered into a contest by paying a real-money entry fee. The skill-based team selection process legitimises the financial transaction in the user's mind, making the entry fee feel like a sporting investment rather than a monetary wager on an uncertain outcome.
💰
Paid Contest Tiers With Real Cash Prizes
Fantasy Power 11 structures its contests across multiple entry-fee tiers — from low-cost contests accessible to new users through to higher-stakes competitions with larger prize pools. This tiered structure creates a natural escalation pathway where a child who wins in a lower tier is encouraged to enter higher-stakes contests, gradually normalising larger financial commitments and introducing the same loss-chasing dynamics found in traditional gambling environments.
📊
Live Match Performance Scoring Creating Unpredictable Outcomes
Fantasy Power 11 contest results depend on real player performances during live matches — introducing a fundamental element of unpredictability that no level of sports knowledge can fully overcome. Weather, injuries, umpiring decisions and team tactics can all alter a player's statistical output in ways that are impossible to predict reliably, meaning that even the most knowledgeable user is exposed to a meaningful element of chance in every paid contest they enter.
💳
In-App Wallet for Deposits and Prize Withdrawals
Fantasy Power 11 operates through an in-app wallet funded with real money via UPI, net banking or linked payment cards. Contest entry fees are deducted from this wallet balance, and prize winnings are credited back to it for withdrawal to a bank account. Once a wallet is funded, a child can enter multiple paid contests without returning to a payment screen — creating a low-friction environment where financial transactions occur repeatedly within a single session with no mandatory parental confirmation.
🔓
No Mandatory Parental Approval for Financial Transactions
Fantasy Power 11 does not require parental confirmation before a child deposits funds or pays contest entry fees. A child with access to a linked payment method or a funded wallet balance can conduct multiple real-money contest entries within any session with no notification sent to a parent and no approval required at each transaction point.

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from real-money fantasy sports contests, financial transactions embedded in sports-themed gameplay, betting-adjacent mechanics dressed in the language of skill and knowledge, and competitive structures that encourage escalating financial involvement.

💸
Exposure to Fantasy Sports Contests Involving Money
Fantasy Power 11's paid contests require real money to enter and distribute cash based on outcomes. For children who associate the app with sports knowledge and team selection, the financial nature of each contest entry is easily overlooked — until meaningful spending has already accumulated
💳
Financial Transactions Connected to Gameplay
Wallet deposits and contest entry fees are genuine financial transactions that can occur repeatedly within a single session with no mandatory parental notification. A child can spend real money across multiple contest entries before a parent has any visibility into what has been transacted
🎰
Sports Betting-Like Mechanics Within a Gaming Interface
Fantasy sports contests combine paid entry, performance-linked uncertain outcomes and cash prize distribution — the functional equivalent of sports betting mechanics, presented within a gaming interface that frames participation as a demonstration of sporting knowledge rather than a financial wager
🔁
Encouragement of Excessive Competitive Gaming
Multiple matches across different sports run simultaneously, with fresh contests available for each fixture. This creates an ongoing environment of paid contest opportunities that rewards continued platform engagement — making it difficult for younger users to set natural limits on their participation and spending
📉
Children May Not Understand the Full Financial Risk
The sports knowledge component of fantasy team selection creates a misleading impression of control over outcomes. Children may believe their cricketing expertise makes contest success reliably achievable — underestimating the role of chance and overestimating the predictability of live match performance as a basis for financial returns
📈
Tiered Entry Creates an Escalation Pathway
The platform's contest tiers — from small-stake practice contests to larger prize pool competitions — create a structured pathway from low financial commitment to higher-stakes participation, normalising escalating spending levels in a way that mirrors the progression mechanics of gambling products designed for adult users

Fantasy Sports Platforms May Be Misunderstood as Skill-Based Games — But They Carry the Same Financial Risks as Other Real-Money Wagering Products
The "skill" framing of fantasy sports — selecting players based on cricket or football knowledge — is central to how these platforms distinguish themselves from gambling products in legal and marketing terms. In practice, the financial mechanics are functionally equivalent: a fee is paid to enter, the outcome depends in part on unpredictable real-world events, and cash is distributed to top performers. Children who understand fantasy sports as a test of sporting knowledge may not recognise that they are participating in a real-money contest with the same loss potential as any other wagering activity — making parental awareness and platform-level controls particularly important for this category of app.

What Parents Should Know

1
Fantasy Sports Platforms May Include Contests That Involve Real Money
Fantasy Power 11 is not a sports trivia game or a free prediction competition — it is a real-money contest platform where entry fees are paid and cash prizes are distributed based on live match performance. Many parents are familiar with fantasy sports as a casual, social activity and may not recognise that the platform their child is using involves genuine financial transactions with each contest entry. Parents should understand that the paid contest structure is the platform's primary product, not a secondary feature — and that a child who is active on the platform is likely conducting regular financial transactions.
2
Children May Not Fully Understand the Financial Risks Associated With These Platforms
Children who are passionate about cricket or football may approach Fantasy Power 11 as a natural extension of their sporting interest — focusing on the team selection challenge rather than the financial structure of the contests they are entering. This framing makes it significantly harder for younger users to recognise that their sports knowledge does not reliably translate into contest success, that each entry fee represents a real and potentially unrecovered financial loss, and that the cumulative cost of regular contest participation can be substantial even when individual entry fees appear small.
3
The Skill Framing Does Not Eliminate the Financial Risk of Each Contest
Fantasy Power 11 is marketed on the basis that team selection is a skill that gives knowledgeable users a genuine advantage over others. While sporting knowledge does influence outcomes, every paid contest also depends on live match performance — an inherently unpredictable variable that no degree of prior knowledge can fully control. A child who believes their cricket expertise makes their contest entries financially reliable is likely to underestimate the loss potential of each entry and overestimate the predictability of their returns across a series of contests.
4
Monitor Access and Review Payment Methods Available on Your Child's Device
In addition to applying iLimits restrictions, parents should review which payment methods are stored or accessible on their child's device and consider removing linked cards or enabling purchase confirmation requirements at the device or app store level. Fantasy Power 11's in-app wallet means that once funds are deposited, all subsequent contest entries can be completed without returning to a payment screen — making it possible for a child to enter multiple paid contests across several match fixtures within a single session, with no mandatory notification or approval required at any point in that process.

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting financial and sporting awareness around fantasy sports platforms, responsible gaming habits and the real money involved in paid contests.

🗣️
Talk With Children About Responsible Gaming Habits
Help your child understand the difference between enjoying sports and participating in real-money fantasy contests — that building a team on Fantasy Power 11 and entering it into a paid competition is a financial transaction, not simply a game. Explain that loving cricket or football is not the same as having the ability to predict live match performance reliably enough to generate consistent financial returns, and that every paid contest carries a genuine risk of losing the entry fee. Discuss how the platform's tiered contest structure is specifically designed to encourage greater financial commitment over time, and establish clear household rules about real-money gaming platforms before your child installs anything new — with an explanation of the reasoning behind those rules, not just the restriction itself.
💰
Explain the Risks of Spending Money Within Gaming Platforms
Talk with your child about how the financial structure of Fantasy Power 11 actually works — that entry fees are paid from a real wallet balance, that prizes are distributed only to top performers and that the majority of paid contest participants lose their entry fees in any given fixture. Discuss the loss-chasing impulse specifically: the powerful feeling of wanting to enter the next contest to recover a previous loss, and why that impulse consistently leads to greater cumulative spending rather than recovered money. Children who understand the mathematical reality of how prize distribution works across all paid entries — not just the advertised prize amounts — are significantly better positioned to make sound decisions about whether and how to engage with platforms of this kind.
🔒
Use Parental Control Tools to Manage Access to Fantasy Sports Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions across every device your child uses — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Alongside blocking Fantasy Power 11 through iLimits, review all payment methods stored on your child's device and consider enabling purchase confirmation or parental approval requirements at the app store or device level to add a step between your child and any financial transaction. The most effective protection combines device-level access controls, payment method management and clear, ongoing conversations about why real-money gaming platforms — including fantasy sports — are restricted for younger users. Children who understand the reasoning behind a restriction are significantly more likely to respect it than those for whom it arrives without explanation.
