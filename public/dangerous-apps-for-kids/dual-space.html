◧
A Completely Separate Second Space on the Device
Unlike simple app copying tools, Dual Space is architecturally built around the concept of a parallel device environment. The "second space" it creates is not just an isolated app instance — it is a separate operational layer where all cloned apps run together, share no data with the primary environment, and are collectively invisible to anyone browsing the standard device. The two spaces coexist on the same physical device but present no information about each other.
📲
Clone Any App Into the Second Space With a Separate Account
Dual Space can duplicate any app installed on the device into its isolated second space. Each clone runs with a completely independent account — its own login credentials, contacts, message history and activity — with no shared data with the original. A child can use an entirely different profile on any messaging app, social platform or game in Dual Space's environment, invisible to anyone examining the primary space.
🔔
Independent Notification Streams Per Space
The Dual Space environment manages its own notifications independently from the primary space. Incoming messages, activity alerts and other notifications on cloned apps within Dual Space do not surface in the standard notification bar alongside the primary apps' alerts. A parent checking the device's lock screen or notification panel sees only the primary space's notification activity, with no indication that a second stream of communications is occurring in parallel.
🗂️
Organised Dual-Account Management Interface
Dual Space provides a dedicated interface for managing all cloned apps within the second space — making it easy to organise, launch and switch between duplicated apps and their independent accounts. For a child, this creates a well-organised hub for maintaining multiple hidden accounts across different platforms simultaneously, with all the tools needed to sustain that activity reliably over time from a single managed environment.
🌐
No Cross-Space Data Sharing
By design, Dual Space enforces strict separation between the primary space and the second space — no contacts, media, messages, login credentials or activity data are shared between the two environments. This means the content of the second space cannot be discovered by examining the primary space's apps, files or account settings. The only way to see what is in the second space is to open Dual Space itself and navigate the cloned environment directly.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
App Cloning Tools Let Children Run a Separate Online Life on the Same Device
Dual Space and similar cloning tools are marketed for adults who need to manage multiple accounts across professional and personal contexts. For children, the practical purpose is almost always to create a hidden version of their online presence — running known, visible accounts in the primary space alongside secondary accounts in the Dual Space environment that parents are not aware of. Because the two spaces share no data by design, the secondary presence is not just difficult to find: it is structurally separated from anything a parent inspecting the device would see.
2
The Second Space Is Specifically Designed So That Examining the Primary Space Reveals Nothing
Dual Space's no-cross-space data-sharing architecture means that standard device review approaches — checking installed app accounts, reviewing notification history, browsing app settings or examining message threads — will find no trace of the second space's contents. The boundary between the two environments is enforced by the app's design, not by a child manually hiding files. A parent who has recently reviewed the device's primary apps may have a completely accurate picture of that environment while the second space operates entirely undiscovered alongside it.
3
Children May Use Dual Space to Maintain Accounts They Know Would Be Restricted
Dual Space is sometimes used by children specifically for apps or account types that a parent has already discussed or restricted in the primary environment. A child who has been asked not to use a particular platform may set up an account for it within Dual Space, where it exists entirely outside the monitored primary space. This pattern is worth considering when a conversation about Dual Space's presence on a device takes place — the secondary account may be for something the child already knows is a concern.
4
Blocking Before Installation Is the Only Fully Reliable Approach
Because the second space is architecturally invisible from the primary space, attempting to detect and investigate Dual Space activity after the app has been installed requires knowing where to look and how to navigate the second space directly — which most parents will not naturally know to do. Device-level restrictions that prevent Dual Space from being installed at all are significantly more reliable than attempting to find something that is specifically designed not to be visible from the environment a parent is most likely to inspect.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to address dual-space tools on your child's device and build a lasting foundation of honest, transparent online behaviour.

💬
Encourage Open Discussions About Responsible Online Behaviour
If you find Dual Space on your child's device, begin with a calm and genuinely curious conversation rather than an immediate confrontation. Ask what was running in the second space and what platforms or accounts were being used there. Because the second space is architecturally separate from the primary environment, its contents are unlikely to have surfaced accidentally — a child using Dual Space has typically made deliberate choices about what to put there and why. Understanding those choices — the platforms, the contacts, the reasons for keeping it private — gives you the right foundation for a meaningful conversation about online safety, trust and the risks of maintaining hidden accounts.
🧭
Explain the Risks of Maintaining Hidden or Secondary Accounts
Help your child understand that the risks of the Dual Space environment are not just about what parents can see — they are about what the child themselves may encounter there. Accounts operating in a hidden second space typically involve less caution, different contacts, and fewer of the natural social safeguards that protect children in their primary online environment. A messaging conversation that a child would handle carefully in their visible account may be managed very differently in an environment they believe no one can reach. The second space is not just private from parents — it is a context where a child is operating with less protection and less accountability than in the environment they are careful about.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to App Cloning Tools
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses, including older or secondary phones. Dual Space's second environment is specifically designed to be invisible from primary space inspection — making detection-based approaches to oversight fundamentally unreliable against this type of tool. Category-level blocking in iLimits prevents Dual Space and similar dual-environment and app cloning tools from being installed before a second space can be established. Combine this with regular honest conversations about which platforms and accounts your child is using so that your oversight covers both the device layer and the broader relationship of trust and transparency that makes all of it work.
