📡 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear picture of MICO Live — a live streaming and social networking platform that combines public broadcasting with real-time audience interaction and stranger discovery, creating a layered environment of potential risk for younger users.

🔴
MICO Live Combines Live Broadcasting With Social Networking — Removing Every Barrier Between a Child and Unknown Adults
MICO Live is not just a live streaming app — it merges live video broadcasting with social discovery and networking features, creating an environment where children can simultaneously broadcast themselves, be discovered by strangers and build ongoing social connections with people they have never met. This combination of features compounds the risk of each individual component: a child is not only on camera in front of an unknown audience, but is also embedded in a social platform designed to sustain and deepen those connections over time.

MICO Live is a live streaming and social networking platform that allows users to broadcast live video to a public global audience and interact with viewers in real time through open chat and virtual gifting. Beyond live streaming, MICO Live also functions as a social discovery app — enabling users to find, follow and communicate with strangers through a combined feed of live content and social profiles. The platform provides no meaningful moderation of live content and no verification of user identity or age.
🔴
Public Live Video Broadcasting to an Unknown Global Audience
MICO Live allows any user to broadcast live video of themselves publicly to a global audience instantly — with no pre-approval, no identity verification and no content review before a stream begins. A child can be live on camera in front of an unlimited number of unknown adults within seconds of opening the app, with no control over who is watching or how that audience is assembled.
💬
Open Live Chat During Broadcasts
All live streams on MICO Live include an open chat feature where viewers can send messages directly to the broadcaster in real time. There is no content filter on live chat, no requirement for a broadcaster to approve messages before they appear on screen, and no effective moderation of what any viewer chooses to write. A child broadcasting live is exposed to whatever an anonymous global audience chooses to send.
🎁
Virtual Gifting and Audience Engagement Mechanics
MICO Live includes a virtual gift system where viewers send gifts to broadcasters during live streams. These mechanics create social and financial incentives for children to continue broadcasting — rewarding prolonged public self-exposure with visible, real-time attention from an unknown audience. Gifting also enables unknown adults to direct sustained, targeted attention toward a specific child broadcaster.
🌐
Social Networking and Stranger Discovery Beyond Live Streams
Unlike standalone streaming apps, MICO Live also functions as a social discovery platform — allowing users to browse profiles, follow strangers and build ongoing social connections outside of live broadcasts. This means a child is not only at risk during a stream, but remains embedded in an environment designed to deepen their contact with unknown individuals across the entire platform experience.
🔓
No Meaningful Age Verification
MICO Live requires only a self-reported date of birth with no verification of the information provided. A child gains full access to the platform — including the ability to broadcast live to a global public audience and interact with any adult user through social networking features — within moments of installing the app. Adults can equally claim any age, with nothing in place to challenge what they state.
⚠️ Section 2

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from public live broadcasting, unfiltered audience interaction, exposure to inappropriate streams, privacy dangers and a social networking layer that sustains and deepens stranger contact over time.

📺
Exposure to Inappropriate Live Streams
MICO Live's discovery feed surfaces streams from around the world with no pre-screening. Children browsing the platform may encounter adult, sexually explicit or otherwise harmful live content before any moderation action is possible
👥
Interaction With Unknown Viewers
Any adult globally can watch a child's live stream and interact through open live chat — creating direct, real-time contact with unknown individuals whose identity, age and intentions cannot be verified by the child or their parents
💬
Inappropriate Live Chat Content
Open live chat is unmoderated and unfiltered. A child broadcasting can receive inappropriate, explicit or threatening messages from viewers in real time, with no delay between a message being sent and it appearing on the child's screen
🔒
Privacy Risks During Broadcasts
A child on camera may inadvertently reveal their location, school, home environment or daily routine — providing unknown viewers with enough personal detail to identify or locate them in the physical world without the child's awareness
🌐
Social Networking Deepens Stranger Contact
MICO Live's social networking layer allows unknown adults who encounter a child through a live stream to follow their profile, initiate private messages and build an ongoing relationship — extending risk well beyond the duration of any individual broadcast
🎁
Gifting Mechanics Reward Prolonged Exposure
Virtual gifts create incentives for children to broadcast more frequently and for longer — rewarding public self-exposure with visible attention and enabling unknown adults to use gifts as a tool to build familiarity and ongoing contact with a specific child
🟠
MICO Live Carries a Medium–High Risk Rating The combination of public live broadcasting, open unmoderated chat, virtual gifting mechanics, social networking features designed to sustain stranger contact, and no meaningful age verification places MICO Live in an elevated risk category for younger users.
📡
Live Streaming Plus Social Networking Creates a Risk Environment That Extends Far Beyond Any Single Broadcast
On a standalone streaming app, the risk is largely contained to the duration of a live session. MICO Live's integrated social networking features mean that unknown adults who discover a child through a live stream can follow them, message them and build an ongoing relationship across the entire platform — long after any individual broadcast has ended. This makes MICO Live meaningfully more risky than a streaming-only platform, and makes device-level controls significantly more important as a protective measure for families.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Live Streaming Puts Children on Camera in Front of a Public Audience With No Safety Net
When a child broadcasts on MICO Live, they are placing themselves live on camera in front of anyone on the platform who chooses to watch — with no control over who that audience is, no way to know whether viewers are adults or peers, and no mechanism to prevent unknown individuals from watching repeatedly. The moment a stream begins, the broadcaster is visible to an unknown global audience and what they share cannot be retrieved or undone.
2
Children May Encounter Inappropriate Content While Browsing
A child who uses MICO Live as a viewer — without broadcasting — is still at risk from the platform's discovery feed, which surfaces live streams from around the world with no meaningful pre-moderation. A child scrolling through available streams can encounter adult, sexually explicit or otherwise harmful live content at any point, with no content warning, no filter and no delay between the content appearing and the child seeing it.
3
Social Networking Features Allow Strangers to Pursue Ongoing Contact After a Stream
Unlike a streaming-only platform, MICO Live allows users who encounter a child during a live broadcast to follow their profile and initiate private messaging — creating a pathway for an unknown adult to build a sustained, private relationship with a child that continues long after any individual stream has ended. This makes MICO Live's risk profile meaningfully more complex than that of a platform focused solely on live content.
4
Live Broadcasts Leave No Record That Parents Can Review
A live stream on MICO Live is a real-time event — when it ends, there is no accessible log of what was shown, said or received in chat that a parent can review after the fact. A child can have a harmful or distressing experience during a broadcast and close the app within seconds, leaving no trace of what occurred. Regular, open conversation with your child about what they encounter on the platform remains the only realistic monitoring tool available for parents of live streaming app users.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around live streaming platforms, public self-exposure and responsible digital behaviour.

🎥
Talk With Children About Responsible Use of Live Streaming Platforms
Help your child understand what live streaming on a platform like MICO Live actually means — that broadcasting places them on camera in front of a public, global audience of people they do not know, with no control over who is watching, no moderation of what viewers say to them and no way to undo what has already been shared. Explain that MICO Live is not just a streaming app — it also functions as a social network that allows unknown individuals to follow and message them after a stream has ended. Establish clear household rules about which platforms are appropriate, and make sure your child understands the reasoning before they install something new.
🤝
Encourage Safe Online Behaviour and Privacy Awareness
Talk regularly with your child about what is and is not safe to share online — particularly in the context of live video, where personal details, home environments, school uniforms and location clues can be visible without a child realising what they are revealing. Discuss the specific risk that live streams and social profiles on MICO Live are publicly discoverable, that viewers may screenshot or record content, and that virtual gifts from unknown adults do not indicate genuine friendship or safe intentions. Children who understand why privacy matters are significantly more likely to protect it, even in situations where you cannot be present.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to Live Streaming Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child has access to — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Live streaming and social networking apps like MICO Live are frequently reinstalled quickly after being removed from a primary device, making device-level controls significantly more reliable than manual monitoring or app-by-app deletion. The strongest protection available to families comes from pairing clear, consistent access controls with a child who genuinely understands why those controls are in place — and feels comfortable coming to you when something online makes them uncomfortable.

No parental control tool can replace the protection that comes from a child who understands why certain platforms are unsafe and feels comfortable talking to their parents about what they encounter online. iLimits gives you the technical layer — the conversations, trust and awareness your family builds together provide the rest.
