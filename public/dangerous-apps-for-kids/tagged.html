What This App Does

A clear picture of Tagged's stranger-social design, features and why its open community poses risks for younger users.

🌐
Tagged Is Built Around Connecting With Strangers
Unlike many social platforms centred on known contacts, Tagged's core design is about discovering and connecting with entirely unknown people worldwide. There is no requirement for mutual knowledge — any user can browse any other user's profile and initiate contact, creating an open social environment with very limited gatekeeping.

Tagged is a social networking and social discovery platform where users meet new people, chat with strangers, play social games and build connections with users around the world. The platform blends dating-style features with casual social interaction — creating an open environment where unknown adults can contact and connect with anyone on the platform.
🏷️
Open Profile Discovery
Any user can browse any other user's profile without a mutual connection or follow requirement. Children using the platform are fully visible to adult strangers worldwide from the moment their profile is created.
💞
Meet Me — Swipe-Style Matching
Tagged includes a "Meet Me" feature where users swipe on or rate other profiles. Mutual interest opens a direct messaging connection between strangers — including between adults and any minor who may have created an account.
🎮
Social Games as a Connection Vector
Tagged incorporates social mini-games designed to encourage interaction between users. These games are used as a low-friction entry point to connect strangers — including children — with adult users through seemingly harmless gameplay.
🎥
Live Video Streaming
Users can broadcast live video to the Tagged community and receive real-time comments and virtual gifts. Live content is largely unmoderated and exposes viewers to adult behaviour and conversations without warning.
💬
Unmoderated Private Messaging
Any user can message any other user directly — no mutual follow or match is required for initial contact. Private messages are completely unscreened with no parental visibility or content moderation.
💎
Virtual Gifts & In-App Purchases
Tagged's live stream feature uses a virtual gifting system purchasable with real money. Children with access to a parent's device or stored payment details may make in-app purchases without parental awareness.
🔞
Tagged Requires Users to Be 18 or Older The platform's open stranger discovery, Meet Me matching, live video and unmoderated messaging are designed for adult social interaction. There is no child-safe mode, content filtering or age-restricted experience tier.
🎮
Social Games Lower a Child's Guard
Tagged's social games are uniquely effective at drawing younger users in — games feel familiar and safe, making it easy for children to begin interacting with unknown adults without recognising that they have entered an adult social and dating environment. This is a risk specific to Tagged that most other dating apps do not carry.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Tagged Allows Users to Connect Freely With Strangers
Unlike social platforms built around existing relationships, Tagged's design actively encourages users to meet and interact with people they have never encountered before. This open-contact model, combined with adult-oriented social and dating features, makes the platform unsuitable for children at any age.
2
The Social Games Feature Is Particularly Deceptive
Tagged's social games are a genuinely unique risk. Because games feel familiar, age-neutral and inherently safe to children, they lower defences in a way that a clear dating profile might not. A child playing a game on Tagged is still interacting with unknown adults in an adult social and dating environment — regardless of how innocuous the activity seems.
3
Younger Users May Install It Out of Curiosity
Children may download Tagged because it appears to be a general social platform rather than a dating app — its blend of social games, live video and casual profiles can make it seem less explicitly adult-focused than other apps in this category. This surface-level impression masks its open-contact adult environment.
4
Age Verification Is Simple to Bypass
Tagged requires only a date of birth and email address to register. A child can create a full account by entering a false birthdate in seconds, immediately gaining access to the complete platform — including open profile discovery, Meet Me matching and direct messaging from unknown adults.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting digital awareness.

💬
Talk With Children About Online Privacy and Safety
Have open, age-appropriate conversations about why platforms like Tagged — which allow any stranger to view profiles and send messages — carry different risks than closed social apps with known contacts. Help children understand that social games and casual features do not make an adult platform safe, and that their personal information and photos are visible to everyone on the platform the moment they sign up.
🤝
Encourage Interaction Within Trusted Social Circles
Help your child invest in and build on the friendships and family connections they already have, rather than seeking contact with unknown adults on open-access platforms. Children with strong, trusted real-world relationships are far better protected against the appeal of anonymous social discovery apps.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Limit Access to Social Discovery Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across all devices your child uses — including tablets, spare phones and shared family devices. Review in-app purchase settings to prevent virtual gift purchases. Revisit your controls regularly and keep honest conversations going alongside them for the most complete layer of protection.
