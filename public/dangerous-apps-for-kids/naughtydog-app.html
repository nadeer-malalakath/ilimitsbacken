What Parents Should Know

1
Adult Chat and Dating Platforms Are Designed for Mature Audiences and Place Children in Contact With Unknown Adults
NaughtyDog is designed for adult social discovery and dating — not for general social use by people of all ages. Every feature of the platform is oriented toward facilitating new connections between adults, and the platform's social environment is shaped by adult norms and adult intentions. A child who accesses the platform is not entering a youth-friendly space that happens to have some adult users — they are entering a platform where every other user has joined specifically to meet and communicate with adults, and where the child's presence is entirely invisible to the platform's safety design because children are not supposed to be there at all.
2
Children May Encounter Conversations, Behaviour or Content That Is Not Appropriate for Their Age
The conversations that take place on adult chat and dating platforms are shaped by the platform's adult social context — mature themes, explicit language, discussions of adult relationships and sexual content are all routine features of the communication environment that a child would encounter. Children who access NaughtyDog may receive unsolicited messages from adult strangers, encounter explicit conversation content, receive inappropriate images or be subjected to communication that is manipulative or harmful. None of these encounters requires an adult user to have harmful intent — they may simply reflect the normal adult social behaviour of the platform's user base responding to what they believe is another adult user.
3
Grooming Risk Is Elevated on Adult-Oriented Platforms With Private Messaging
Individuals who seek to harm children online actively use adult dating and chat platforms to find and communicate with young people who have accessed the platform without their parents' awareness. The private messaging features of platforms like NaughtyDog provide the unmonitored communication channel that grooming typically requires — a way to build a private relationship with a child over time, to establish trust, to request personal information and, eventually, to encourage meetings or solicit images. Parents should be aware that a child's presence on a platform like NaughtyDog may not be visible on the device at all if the app has been hidden or if the child is accessing the platform through a browser rather than a dedicated app.
4
Parents May Want to Monitor or Restrict Access and Have an Open Conversation With Their Child
Restricting access to NaughtyDog is the immediate protective step — but the most durable protection comes from a child who understands why adult chat and dating platforms are not appropriate for them, and who feels comfortable coming to a parent if they have encountered something on the platform or if they have been contacted by someone in a way that concerned or confused them. Children who have discovered the platform may be reluctant to disclose this to a parent for fear of punishment — an approach that frames the conversation around safety rather than rule-breaking will produce a more open response and a better outcome for the child's ongoing wellbeing.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child from adult chat and dating platforms and build lasting awareness around online safety, responsible digital communication and the risks of stranger contact.

💬
Talk Openly With Children About Online Safety and Stranger Interactions
Help your child understand clearly and specifically what makes adult chat and dating platforms unsafe for them — not in abstract terms, but concretely: that the platform is designed for adults to meet other adults, that every user on the platform is a stranger whose identity and intentions cannot be verified, and that some adults specifically use these platforms to find and communicate with children who have gained access to them. Make clear that an adult who initiates contact on a platform like this and seems friendly, understanding or interested in the child's life is not necessarily safe — and that building a private online relationship with an unknown adult is always a situation to tell a parent about. Reinforce that coming to you will not result in punishment but in support.
🤝
Encourage Responsible Digital Communication
Help your child develop clear, practical habits around who they communicate with online and what information they share in digital conversations. The core principles are simple and worth stating explicitly: never share your real name, school, address or location with someone you have not met in person and whose identity has not been confirmed by a trusted adult; never move a conversation from a known platform to a private channel at the request of someone you only know online; and always tell a trusted adult if someone online makes you feel uncomfortable, asks you to keep conversations secret, or requests anything that feels unusual or inappropriate. These habits — practised and normalised through regular, calm conversation — provide protection that no technical control alone can replicate.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Restrict Access to Adult Chat and Dating Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions immediately and consistently across every device your child has access to, including tablets, secondary phones and shared household devices. Because adult chat platforms are often accessible through browsers as well as dedicated apps, browser-level controls should be applied in addition to app-level restrictions. Review app installations on your child's device periodically — adult-oriented apps are sometimes hidden using disguised icons or moved to folders that are less likely to be noticed casually. If you discover that your child has already been using a platform like NaughtyDog, approach the conversation with a focus on their safety and wellbeing rather than the breach of rules, and consider whether reporting the platform to iLimits will help protect other families too.
🔐

The Safest Children Online Are Those Who Know They Can Talk to a Parent

Technical restrictions are the first and fastest line of defence against adult chat and dating platforms — but the deepest protection comes from a child who understands why these platforms are dangerous, who has the vocabulary to recognise concerning online behaviour, and who trusts completely that bringing a concern to a parent will be met with support rather than blame. iLimits gives you the tools; the trust you build at home gives your child the confidence to use them.
