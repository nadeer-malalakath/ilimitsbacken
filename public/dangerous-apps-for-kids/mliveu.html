What This App Does

A clear picture of MLiveU — a live streaming and social video platform that lets anyone broadcast themselves publicly to an unknown global audience, with open real-time interaction between streamers and viewers and no meaningful gatekeeping for younger users.

🔴
MLiveU Connects Users Live on Camera With a Global Public Audience — Instantly and Without Screening
MLiveU is designed for speed and immediacy — the path from installing the app to broadcasting live video to strangers worldwide is as short as the platform can make it. There is no pre-approval process, no content review before a stream goes live, and no identity check before a child is on camera in front of an unknown public audience. Simultaneously, any viewer anywhere in the world can interact with that child in real time through open live chat — with no filter and no moderation standing between an anonymous adult and a broadcasting child.

A child broadcasting on MLiveU may attract an audience well beyond their awareness, with no control over who finds, watches or follows their stream.

No Meaningful Age Verification

A child gains full access to the platform — including the ability to broadcast themselves live to a global public audience — within moments of installing the app. Adults can equally state any age, with nothing in place to challenge what they claim.
⚠️ Section 2

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from public live broadcasting, unfiltered viewer interaction, exposure to inappropriate stream content, privacy dangers during broadcasts and platform mechanics that incentivise prolonged public self-exposure.

📺
Exposure to Inappropriate Live Streams
MLiveU's discovery feed surfaces streams from around the world with no pre-screening. Children browsing the platform can encounter adult, sexually explicit or otherwise harmful live content before any moderation is possible — with no content warning and no age filter
👥
Interaction With Unknown Viewers
Any adult globally can watch a child's live stream without restriction and interact through open live chat — creating direct real-time contact between a child on camera and unknown individuals whose identity, age and intentions are entirely unverified
💬
Inappropriate Live Chat Conversations
Open live chat during broadcasts is unmoderated and unfiltered. A child broadcasting live can receive inappropriate, explicit or threatening messages from viewers in real time, with no buffer between a message being sent and the child seeing it appear on screen
🔒
Privacy Risks During Broadcasts
A child broadcasting live may inadvertently reveal their location, school, home environment or daily routine — providing unknown viewers with personal information sufficient to identify or locate them in the real world, often without the child being aware of what they are sharing
🎭
Grooming Risk via Gifts and Attention
Adults can use virtual gifts to direct sustained, targeted attention toward a specific child broadcaster — building familiarity and a sense of obligation through repeated engagement that may feel like genuine appreciation but functions as a relationship-building mechanism
📍
Public Discoverability Without Consent
Children who broadcast on MLiveU are publicly discoverable by anyone on the platform. Unknown adults can find, follow and watch a child's streams repeatedly — building familiarity with someone who is, from the child's perspective, a complete stranger

Live Streaming Places a Child on Camera in Front of Unknown Adults in Real Time — With No Safety Net
Unlike social media posts that can be deleted or messages that can be blocked, a live broadcast on MLiveU happens in real time and cannot be undone once it has occurred. A child who streams on this platform is placing themselves on camera in front of a public, unvetted global audience — with no moderator present, no content filter active and no effective mechanism available to protect them if something goes wrong during the session. The irreversibility of live content, combined with the openness of the platform's audience, is precisely what makes MLiveU a meaningful risk for younger users.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Live Streaming Platforms Expose Children to a Public, Uncontrolled Audience
When a child broadcasts on MLiveU, they are on live camera in front of anyone on the platform who chooses to watch — with no control over who that audience is, no way of knowing whether viewers are adults or peers, and no mechanism to prevent unknown individuals from repeatedly watching their streams. The moment a broadcast begins, the child is visible to a global audience and the content of what they share cannot be retrieved.
2
Children May Encounter Inappropriate Content While Browsing
Even a child who never broadcasts on MLiveU is at risk simply from using the app as a viewer. The discovery feed surfaces live streams from around the world with no meaningful pre-moderation — a child scrolling through available streams may encounter adult, sexually explicit or otherwise harmful content at any point, with no content warning, no age filter and no delay between inappropriate content appearing and the child being exposed to it.
3
Live Broadcasts Leave No Record That Parents Can Review
A live stream on MLiveU is a real-time event — when it ends, there is no accessible log of what was shown, said or received in chat that a parent can review after the fact. A child can have a distressing or dangerous experience during a broadcast and close the app within seconds, with nothing logged, no notification sent and no record of what occurred available to a parent. Open, regular conversation with your child about what they encounter online is the only realistic monitoring tool available in the absence of device-level controls.
4
Gift Mechanics Incentivise Children to Broadcast More and for Longer
MLiveU's virtual gift system creates a direct incentive for children to stream more frequently, for longer periods and to a larger audience — because gifts come from viewers, and more viewers mean more gifts. Children are not well-equipped to recognise when an adult's gifting behaviour is being used to build inappropriate familiarity rather than to express genuine appreciation. The platform's reward structure actively works against a younger user's ability to protect themselves in this environment.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around live streaming platforms, public self-exposure and responsible digital behaviour.

🎥
Talk With Children About Responsible Use of Live Streaming Platforms
Help your child understand what live streaming on a platform like MLiveU actually means — that broadcasting places them on camera in front of a public, global audience of people they have never met, with no control over who is watching, no moderation of what viewers say to them and no way to undo what has already been shown or shared. Explain that virtual gifts and attention from unknown viewers do not indicate genuine friendship or safe intentions, and that the casual, entertaining feel of the platform does not change the real-world risks of being on live camera in front of strangers. Establish clear household rules about which platforms are appropriate before your child installs something new, and explain the reasoning — children respond far better to understanding than to unexplained restrictions.
🤝
Encourage Safe Online Behaviour and Privacy Awareness
Talk regularly with your child about what is and is not safe to share online — particularly in the context of live video, where personal details, home environments, school uniforms and location clues can be visible on screen without a child realising what they are revealing to an unknown audience. Discuss the specific risk that live streams can expose far more than a child intends to share, and that viewers may record or screenshot content without the broadcaster's knowledge or consent. Children who understand why privacy matters — and who know what to do if an online interaction makes them feel uncomfortable — are significantly better protected than those who are simply told not to use certain apps.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to Live Streaming Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child has access to — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Live streaming apps like MLiveU are frequently reinstalled within minutes of being removed from a primary device, making device-level controls significantly more reliable than app-by-app deletion or manual monitoring alone. The strongest protection available to families comes from combining clear, consistent access controls with honest, ongoing conversations about why those controls are in place — and building an environment where your child feels comfortable coming to you if something online makes them uncomfortable.
