What Parents Should Know

1
Online Poker Platforms Are Designed for Adult Audiences and Typically Involve Real-Money Wagers
PokerStars is a regulated gambling product licensed for adult use — it is not a general-audience gaming app that happens to include financial elements. The platform's game mechanics, competitive structures and engagement design are calibrated for adult users who are legally permitted to gamble and who are assumed to have the financial maturity and impulse-control capacity to manage the associated risks. A child accessing this platform is accessing a product that was never designed for their age group and that carries the same category of harm risk as any other licensed gambling environment.
2
Children May Not Fully Understand the Risks Associated With Gambling-Style Games
Children who are drawn to PokerStars may approach it through the lens of a card game skill challenge — focusing on learning poker strategy, competing against others and experiencing the excitement of competitive play, without fully recognising the financial and psychological risks embedded in the platform's design. The availability of play money mode may create a misleading impression that the platform is suitable for all users, while simultaneously familiarising younger users with all the mechanics of the real-money gambling environment in preparation for a transition that the platform actively encourages.
3
Early Exposure to Gambling Products Is Associated With Elevated Long-Term Risk
Research on gambling harm consistently identifies early exposure to gambling products as a significant risk factor for the development of problem gambling behaviour in adolescence and adulthood. Children and adolescents are more vulnerable to the addictive mechanics of gambling products than adults — with less developed impulse-control, greater susceptibility to variable reward systems and less financial perspective to contextualise losses. Preventing access to licensed gambling platforms during childhood and adolescence is one of the most effective risk-reduction measures available to parents.
4
Platform Age Verification Does Not Reliably Prevent Under-Age Access
PokerStars is legally required to verify user age before permitting access to real-money gambling — but age verification processes can be bypassed by a determined younger user with access to a parent's identification details or an existing funded account. Parents should not rely on the platform's own verification processes as the primary safeguard against a child accessing the product, and should instead combine iLimits device-level blocking with payment method management and open conversations about gambling platforms and the risks they carry.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around gambling products, responsible gaming habits and the serious risks of adult-regulated platforms reaching younger users.

🗣️
Talk With Children About the Risks of Gambling-Related Games
Help your child understand the clear distinction between playing card games socially and using a licensed gambling platform like PokerStars — that the two share surface-level similarities but operate on entirely different financial and psychological foundations. Explain that PokerStars is a product designed for adults who are legally permitted to gamble, that its mechanics are specifically engineered to make continued play feel compelling, and that the house or other players consistently hold structural advantages that make it impossible to generate reliable financial returns through skill alone. Discuss what gambling is, what regulated gambling platforms are, and why access to them is age-restricted in the same way that alcohol and tobacco are — not as arbitrary rules, but because the harm potential for younger users is well-documented and significant. Make sure your child understands these reasons, not just the restriction.
🤝
Encourage Responsible Gaming Habits Across All Platforms
Use PokerStars as a starting point for a broader, ongoing conversation about gambling awareness and responsible gaming across all digital platforms — not just this one. Discuss how gambling mechanics appear in many different product types, including some that are not regulated as gambling products, and how recognising these mechanics is a valuable skill for navigating digital environments as your child grows older. Establish household norms around gaming — time limits, the distinction between free and paid gameplay, and how to recognise when a gaming environment is designed to maximise spending or continued engagement rather than simply to entertain. Children who develop genuine media literacy around gambling mechanics, and who feel comfortable discussing what they encounter online with a parent, are significantly better protected across all platforms — not just the ones that are clearly flagged as restricted.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Restrict Access to Gambling Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions across every device your child uses — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Alongside blocking PokerStars through iLimits, review all payment methods stored on your child's device, remove saved card details where possible and enable purchase confirmation or parental approval requirements at the device or app store level. Consider also reviewing whether your own PokerStars account, if you have one, is accessible on shared or family devices — a child accessing an existing funded account bypasses the platform's own age verification entirely. The combination of device-level access controls, payment method management and clear, age-appropriate conversations about gambling products provides the strongest protection available to families navigating this category of platform.
