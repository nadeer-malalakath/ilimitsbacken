📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear look at how Hinge works, what makes it distinctive among dating apps, and why it is designed exclusively for adults.

💬
Hinge is "Designed to be Deleted"
Hinge markets itself as a serious adult dating app built for long-term relationships — not casual swiping. Its design philosophy is centred on adult romantic intent, which makes it entirely unsuitable for children.
Hinge is a relationship-focused dating application that helps adults meet and form romantic connections through rich profile interactions and direct messaging. Unlike simple swipe apps, Hinge builds detailed profiles and encourages users to comment on specific content before matching — all in a context designed entirely around adult relationships.
❓
Prompt-Based Profile System
Users answer personal questions and creative prompts — such as "My love language is…" or "I'm looking for…" — which other users like or comment on to initiate connection. All prompts and answers are oriented toward adult romantic and personal life.
🗺️
Location and Proximity Matching
Hinge surfaces potential matches based on the user's real-time location. The app actively uses device location to find nearby users, indirectly sharing geographic information with strangers.
💬
Private Unmoderated Messaging
Once matched, users exchange private messages with no parental oversight, content moderation or time limits. Conversations can include photos, voice notes and links.
📋
Detailed Personal Information Profiles
Hinge profiles include photos, age, height, religion, politics, relationship intent and lifestyle habits — a level of personal disclosure designed for adults navigating compatibility in romantic relationships.
💎
Premium Features for Increased Visibility
Paid tiers unlock features like seeing who has already liked your profile, boosting your visibility and sending additional "roses" to express strong interest — systems designed to maximise engagement and platform spending.
🔞
Hinge Requires Users to Be 18 or Older Every aspect of the platform — its prompts, profiles, messaging and culture — is built around adult romantic life. There is no age-appropriate space within this app for children.
⚠️ Section 2

Why It May Not Be Appropriate for Children

Specific risks that arise when children access apps built around adult dating and intimate relationship formation.

💑
Adult Relationship Context
The platform is entirely designed around adult romantic intent — deeply outside what is appropriate for a child's development
💬
Mature Conversations
Private chats are unmoderated and may include intimate, explicit or adult-only content with no visibility to parents
👤
Unknown Adult Contact
Children can match and communicate with unknown adults who may be unaware of — or unconcerned by — their real age
📍
Location Data Exposure
Proximity-based matching reveals a child's geographic location to a pool of adult strangers in real time
🪞
Self-Image Pressures
Detailed photo and personal-data profiles create comparison pressure that can deeply affect a child's developing sense of self-worth
🎭
Distorted Relationship Norms
Prompt-based matching shapes unrealistic expectations about romantic compatibility and how adult relationships are initiated
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Dating Apps Are Designed for Adults 18 and Above
Hinge's prompts, profiles, culture and messaging exist entirely within the context of adult romantic life. The expectations, norms and conversations on the platform are not suitable for children or teenagers at any stage of development.
2
Curiosity and Peer Influence Are Common Entry Points
Children may download Hinge out of curiosity, because friends use it, or simply to explore what adult social life looks like. This is natural — but once inside the app, the platform's design will not protect them from adult content or interactions.
3
Age Verification Is Simple to Circumvent
Hinge requires a phone number or Apple/Google account for sign-up, with a self-reported birth date. A child can create an account by providing a false age. Platform-level verification is not reliable enough to prevent under-age access.
4
Build Healthy Foundations Before Digital Dating
Children who have clear, values-based conversations with parents about relationships — respect, trust, safety and communication — are far better protected against the risks of adult dating platforms than those whose only exposure comes from the apps themselves.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and help them develop a healthy relationship with technology and relationships.

💬
Discuss Online Relationships and Digital Safety
Have calm, open conversations about what healthy relationships look like — online and offline — and why dating platforms like Hinge exist for adults. Children who feel comfortable raising relationship questions with a parent are far less likely to seek answers through adult apps.
🤝
Encourage Connection With Trusted Friends, Not Strangers Online
Help children invest in relationships they already have — family, close friends, school communities — rather than reaching for connection with unknown adults online. Healthy friendships built on real familiarity are the best foundation for everything that follows.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Limit Access to Adult-Focused Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses. Revisit your settings as they grow and their access to devices changes. Controls establish the boundary — open, trusting conversations lay the foundation beneath it.
