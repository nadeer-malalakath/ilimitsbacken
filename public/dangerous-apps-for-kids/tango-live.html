What This App Does

A clear picture of Tango Live — a live streaming and social entertainment platform where users broadcast themselves publicly to a global audience and engage with viewers in real time through open chat and direct messaging.

🎙️
Tango Live Is Built Around Public Broadcasting and Real-Time Audience Interaction
Tango Live's entire model is built on instant, public live video — any user can begin broadcasting to the world within seconds of opening the app, and any other user can watch and interact with them in real time. There is no buffer between going live and reaching a global audience of strangers, and no content review before a stream is visible to others. This immediacy removes every layer of safety that conventional platforms provide and places children in an uncontrolled live environment the moment they engage with the app.

Tango Live is a live streaming and social entertainment platform that allows users to broadcast live video and interact with viewers in real time. Users can watch live broadcasts, send messages and communicate with streamers or other viewers from around the world. The app focuses on entertainment, social interaction and live video content — all of which occurs without content screening or any parental visibility into who is watching or being watched.
🎥
Instant Public Live Video Broadcasting
Any Tango Live user can begin streaming live video to a global public audience in seconds — with no content review, no age verification of viewers and no moderation before the stream goes live. A child broadcasting on Tango Live is immediately visible to an unlimited number of unknown adults worldwide, with no control over who is watching or what they may write in the live chat.
💬
Open Live Chat Visible to All Viewers
During any live stream, all viewers can post messages directly to the broadcaster in a public chat visible to every other person watching. These messages are entirely unmoderated and contain no content filtering — children broadcasting are exposed to whatever adult viewers choose to write in real time, with no mechanism to review or remove messages before they appear on screen.
🎁
Virtual Gift Economy Tied to Real Money
Tango Live features a virtual gift system where viewers purchase and send digital gifts to broadcasters during live streams, which can be converted into real monetary value. This creates financial incentives for broadcasters to continue performing for their audience and encourages viewers to spend real money. Children on either side of this system — broadcasting to earn gifts or spending to send them — are engaging with adult monetisation mechanics they are not equipped to navigate safely.
✉️
Private Direct Messaging With Any User
After discovering a child through a live stream, any Tango Live user can send them a private direct message. These private conversations are completely hidden from parents, carry no content filtering and create a direct, unmonitored channel between a child and any adult who has watched their stream and chosen to make contact privately.
🔓
No Meaningful Age Verification
Tango Live does not verify the age stated at registration. A child can gain full access to live broadcasting, public audience interaction, private messaging and the virtual gift economy within moments of installing the app, with no meaningful safeguard applied before they are exposed to the platform's full adult user environment.

Key risks from unscreened live broadcasts, real-time interaction with unknown adults, financial gift mechanics and serious privacy concerns arising from public live video streaming.

🔞
Mature or Inappropriate Live Streams
Live content is published with no prior review — children can be exposed to adult-only, sexual or disturbing material in real time with no content warning and no moderation delay between broadcast and viewer
👁️
Unknown Adults Watching Children Broadcast
A child broadcasting live is immediately visible to any adult on the platform — with no restriction on who can watch, interact or follow up with private contact after the stream ends
💬
Inappropriate Live Chat Content
Live chat is unmoderated and fully public — children broadcasting are directly exposed to sexually explicit, harassing or threatening messages from unknown adult viewers in real time
🔒
Privacy Risks From Live Broadcasting
Children who go live may unknowingly reveal identifying information — their face, home environment, school uniform or location — to a global unknown audience with no way to recall what has already been seen
💸
Virtual Gift and Monetisation Pressure
The gift economy can create financial exposure and unhealthy social pressure — children may spend real money sending gifts or feel compelled to continue broadcasting to sustain audience approval and incoming rewards
😟
Grooming Risk via Private Messages

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Live Streaming Is a Fundamentally Different Risk Category to Standard Video Platforms
Parents who allow their child to use video platforms like YouTube may not immediately recognise that live streaming involves a categorically different level of risk. On Tango Live, content is never reviewed before it reaches viewers, the audience is fully interactive and every session is happening in real time with no moderation buffer. A child can encounter explicit content or be directly approached by an adult in a way that is simply not possible on a standard pre-recorded video platform.
2
A Child Broadcasting Live Is Immediately Visible to a Global Adult Audience
When a child goes live on Tango Live, their face, voice, surroundings and any personal details visible in their environment are immediately broadcast to every user on the platform who discovers their stream — with no restriction on who can watch. Adults who discover the stream can interact in live chat, follow the child's account and send private messages once the stream has ended. The child has no way of knowing who is watching or what those individuals intend.
3
The Gift Economy Creates Real Financial and Emotional Risks for Children
Tango Live's virtual gift system is designed to maximise broadcaster engagement and viewer spending. Children who broadcast can develop a dependency on the validation that incoming gifts represent — creating pressure to continue streaming, to please their audience or to present themselves in ways that attract more gifts. Children who view streams may spend real money on gifts without fully understanding that they are making real financial transactions. Both dynamics are designed for adult users and are inappropriate for children.
4
Private Messages From Strangers Are Hidden From Parents by Default
Any adult who watches a child's live stream can immediately send them a private direct message without any mutual follow or connection being required. These private conversations are entirely invisible to parents, carry no content filtering and leave no accessible log. A child may be receiving inappropriate messages from multiple unknown adults without any parent ever becoming aware that contact has been made.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around the risks of live streaming, privacy and responsible digital behaviour.

🎙️
Talk With Children About the Risks of Live Streaming Platforms
Help your child understand that going live means broadcasting to a global unknown audience in real time — not sharing content with friends. Be specific about what should never appear in a live stream: their face alongside identifiable personal details, their home address or school name, their location or daily routine, and any information that could be used to find them. Explain that live streams are permanent in the sense that cannot be undone — once something has been seen by viewers, it has been seen, regardless of whether the stream is later deleted.
🛡️
Encourage Safe Online Behaviour and Privacy Awareness
Have regular conversations with your child about online safety in the specific context of live video — what it means for a stranger to be watching them, why private messages from people they do not know in real life should be treated with caution, and what to do if a live chat or private conversation makes them feel uncomfortable or unsafe. Children who understand the concrete risks of live streaming, rather than treating it as equivalent to watching recorded video, are significantly better positioned to protect themselves when you are not present to intervene.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to Live Streaming Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child can access — including tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices. Live streaming apps are reinstalled quickly and are often discovered through peer recommendations or social media, making device-level controls significantly more reliable than removing individual apps manually. Combine technical restrictions with open, ongoing conversations about why these platforms are not appropriate at this stage, to build the most effective and sustainable protection available for your family.
