📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear picture of SayHi Chat's open-world design and why unrestricted stranger communication is its defining risk.

🌐
SayHi Is Designed for Chatting With Total Strangers
SayHi Chat's entire purpose is connecting users with people they have never met — anyone, anywhere in the world, without mutual consent or verification. Users do not need to follow each other, match, or have any prior connection before messaging begins. This zero-barrier model means a child can be contacted by unknown adults the moment they create a profile.

SayHi Chat is a global stranger-chat and social discovery platform that allows users to meet and communicate with new people from around the world. Users browse profiles, discover people nearby and start conversations through messaging — with no requirement for mutual connection, consent or any form of identity verification.
💬
Zero-Barrier Messaging
Any user can message any other user directly — no match, mutual follow or two-way consent is needed to initiate contact. A child creating a profile is immediately reachable by unknown adults anywhere in the world.
📍
Nearby People Discovery
SayHi surfaces profiles of users in the surrounding geographic area, enabling adults to target and message children based on their physical proximity. A child's approximate location is surfaced to all nearby app users by default.
🌐
Language-Filter Discovery
Users can filter profiles by language, making it easy for adults to specifically seek out and target native-language speakers in any region — including children who may be listed in the same language filter.
👤
Open Profile Visibility
User profiles including photos, age, language and location are visible to all users on the platform without any prior connection. Children who create accounts are fully visible and searchable to unknown adults worldwide from the moment they sign up.
📸
Photo & Media Sharing in Chat
Users can share photos and media files within private conversations. Unmoderated media exchange between children and unknown adults creates significant risk of exposure to inappropriate content and potential grooming.
🔔
Push Notifications Drive Re-Engagement
SayHi sends push notifications when new messages arrive from strangers, actively pulling users back into conversations. For children who have been contacted by unknown adults, these notifications continue to arrive even after closing the app.
🔞
SayHi Chat Is Intended for Users 17 or Older The platform's open stranger-contact model, unmoderated private messaging and global profile visibility are wholly unsuitable for children at any age. There is no child-safe content tier or age-restricted experience available.
🎭
Adults Can Pose as Peers — and Children Cannot Tell the Difference
Stranger-chat apps like SayHi have no identity verification. Adults can create profiles presenting as teenagers or young people the same age as your child. Without the ability to verify who they are actually speaking to, children may share personal information, photos or meet-up plans with adults they believe to be peers. This is a risk that no amount of digital literacy alone can fully eliminate.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Stranger Chat Apps Allow Open Communication With People Children Do Not Know
Unlike social platforms built around existing friendships, SayHi Chat is explicitly designed for communicating with complete strangers. There is no social graph, no mutual connection and no verification layer between your child and any adult user on the platform — globally.
2
Children Cannot Verify Who They Are Speaking To
SayHi applies no identity verification to its users. This means an adult can present as a teenager and build a relationship with a child over time without the child having any way to confirm who they are really communicating with. Digital literacy skills do not protect against this — a child who is aware of online risks can still be deceived by a carefully constructed false profile.
3
Curiosity Is the Most Common Entry Point
Children may download SayHi out of genuine curiosity about meeting people from other countries or cultures — the app's global, language-based discovery feels educational and social on the surface. This framing can mask the platform's lack of safety features and the real risks of its open-contact design.
4
Conversations Can Continue After the App Is Closed
SayHi's push notification system means that once a child has received a message from an unknown adult, contact can continue even when the app is not actively open. Conversations initiated on the platform can also migrate to other messaging services if a child shares their contact details — making early prevention more effective than late intervention.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child from the specific risks of stranger-chat platforms.

💬
Teach Children Not to Communicate With Strangers Online
Have honest, age-appropriate conversations about why communicating with unknown people online carries real risks — even when the other person seems friendly, relatable or appears to be the same age. Help children understand that they cannot verify who they are speaking to on open platforms, and that adults can and do present themselves as peers. Equipping children with this knowledge is essential before they encounter these platforms, not after.
🔐
Discuss Privacy and Personal Information Safety
Talk with your child about what personal information should never be shared with strangers online — including their real name, school, address, phone number and photos. Emphasise that sharing these details on an open-chat platform can have real-world consequences. A child who understands the value of their own privacy is significantly better protected than one who has simply been told not to use certain apps.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Prevent Access to Risky Chat Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses — including older phones, tablets and shared family devices. Stranger-chat apps are particularly easy to reinstall after deletion, so consistent device-level restrictions are more reliable than manual monitoring alone. Combine controls with regular, open family conversations about online safety for the strongest protection.
