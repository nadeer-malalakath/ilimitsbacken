What This App Does

A clear picture of Connected2.me — an anonymous messaging platform that allows anyone on the internet to send messages to a user's open inbox without revealing who they are.

📬
Connected2.me Gives Anyone on the Internet an Open Channel to Message Your Child — Anonymously
Unlike most messaging apps where a user must first be accepted as a contact, Connected2.me creates an open inbox that any stranger can send messages to — without any prior connection and without revealing who they are. A child using the app is effectively making themselves reachable by the entire internet, with no control over who sends them messages or what those messages contain.
🔗
A Shareable Link Gives Unlimited Strangers Direct Access to Message a Child
Connected2.me provides each user with a shareable profile link that can be posted on social media, shared in group chats or distributed publicly. Any person who clicks that link — from anywhere in the world — can immediately send an anonymous message. The wider a child shares this link, the larger and more unpredictable the pool of anonymous strangers who can reach them directly.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Connected2.me Inverts the Normal Model of Online Messaging Safety
Conventional messaging apps are opt-in — a child can only be contacted by people they have accepted. Connected2.me reverses this entirely. By creating an open inbox linked to a shareable public link, the default state is that any stranger on the internet can send a message without any action from the child, without any acceptance step and without revealing who they are. Parents should understand that this is not a privacy loophole — it is the platform's intentional design.
2
The Link Mechanic Means Risk Grows Every Time It Is Shared
Every time a child adds their Connected2.me link to a social profile — Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat, a school group chat — they add a new, permanent pathway for anonymous strangers to reach them. The cumulative risk grows with every share. A child who has posted their link across multiple platforms may be reachable by hundreds or thousands of unknown users — and has no way of knowing who among them has chosen to send a message until it arrives in their inbox.
3
Anonymous Platforms Are Strongly Associated With Cyberbullying
Research consistently shows that platforms which guarantee sender anonymity carry significantly higher rates of harmful, abusive and harassing messages. The absence of any social consequence for what is sent — because the sender cannot be identified — removes every inhibition that would otherwise moderate behaviour. Connected2.me's no-login requirement makes this worse: there is no account to report, no user to block and no record to show a school, parent or authorities in the event of serious harm.
4
Children Are Often Drawn to the Platform by Curiosity — Not Awareness of Risk
Connected2.me and similar anonymous messaging platforms are frequently introduced to children by peers in a spirit of fun — curiosity about what others will say anonymously can feel exciting and socially appealing. Children who join the platform may have no awareness that the same anonymity that makes it feel playful also makes it impossible to screen out harmful messages from unknown adults. By the time a distressing message arrives, the inbox is already open and the link already widely shared.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around anonymous messaging, shareable link risks and responsible digital behaviour.

💬
Teach Children About the Risks of Anonymous Messaging
Have a direct, calm conversation with your child about what it means to have an open anonymous inbox — not just the messages that feel fun or flattering, but the ones that feel upsetting, confusing or frightening, and the fact that there is no way to know which type will arrive next. Help them understand the specific mechanic of Connected2.me: that sharing their link on any platform permanently opens that platform as a channel for unknown strangers, and that deleting the app does not automatically remove links that are already circulating. Make it completely safe for them to show you any messages they receive and to come to you immediately if something in their inbox has distressed or alarmed them.
🤝
Encourage Responsible Digital Behaviour and Privacy Awareness
Talk with your child regularly about why some personal information should never be publicly accessible online — including their face, real name and any link that allows strangers to contact them directly. Help them understand that an anonymous message from an unverified sender cannot be assumed to be safe regardless of what it says, and that the most appropriate response to an unsolicited anonymous message from someone they do not recognise is not to reply. Build a habit of checking, together, what information is publicly visible on each platform they use — including what links appear in their social media bios — and reviewing it periodically as their online presence grows.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Prevent Access to Anonymous Chat Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses — tablets, older phones and any shared household devices. Anonymous messaging apps like Connected2.me can be reinstalled within minutes of deletion, and their names may not be immediately recognisable to a parent conducting a manual review of installed apps. Device-level category blocks are significantly more robust than app-by-app removal. If your child has already shared their Connected2.me link on other platforms, take the time to remove it from each profile together — closing the inbox on the app alone does not remove pre-existing links from other platforms where it was posted.
