📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear picture of MeetMe's large-scale stranger-social platform, live streaming economy and why its open-contact design poses risks for younger users.

📊
MeetMe Has Over 100 Million Registered Users Worldwide
MeetMe is one of the largest social discovery platforms in the world, operating under The Meet Group alongside other apps including Skout, Lovoo and Zoosk. Its scale means that a child creating a profile is immediately exposed to an enormous pool of unknown adults — with no barrier to contact between them. MeetMe's reach across multiple platforms also means that users from its sister apps can interact within the same ecosystem.

MeetMe is a social networking and discovery platform that allows users to meet new people, chat with strangers and participate in live streaming interactions. Users browse profiles, start conversations and connect with people they have never met — within a large-scale open-contact environment that spans over 100 million registered users and is connected to a broader family of social discovery apps.
👤
Open Profile Discovery
MeetMe allows any user to browse any other user's profile without a prior connection. A child's profile — including their photo, age, location and activity — is visible to the platform's entire adult user community from the moment they sign up.
📍
Nearby People Discovery
MeetMe surfaces profiles of users in the surrounding geographic area, making a child's approximate physical location visible to adult strangers nearby. Location-based discovery enables targeted, proximity-based contact between adults and local children.
📺
Live Video Streaming
MeetMe features a prominent live streaming platform where users broadcast video to the community and receive real-time comments and virtual diamond gifts. Streams are largely unmoderated and may expose child viewers to adult conversations, behaviour and content without warning.
💬
Open Unmoderated Private Messaging
Any user can send a private message to any other user without a match or prior connection. Private chats are completely unscreened — there is no content moderation or parental visibility for messages exchanged between children and unknown adults.
💎
Diamonds, Gifts & In-App Economy
MeetMe operates a virtual diamonds and gifts economy used in live streams and profile interactions. Diamonds are purchased using real money through in-app payments. Children with access to a parent's payment method could make purchases without any parental awareness.
🔗
Cross-Platform Ecosystem Exposure
MeetMe operates within The Meet Group's wider ecosystem, which includes Skout, Lovoo and Zoosk. Users across these platforms can interact with each other — significantly expanding the pool of unknown adults a child may be exposed to beyond MeetMe's own user community.
🔞
MeetMe Is Intended for Users 17 or Older The platform's open stranger-contact model, live broadcasting, cross-platform exposure and unmoderated messaging are not appropriate for children at any age. No child-safe content tier or restricted experience is available.
🔗
Cross-Platform Links Mean Exposure Goes Beyond MeetMe Itself
MeetMe's integration with other apps in The Meet Group ecosystem — including Skout and Lovoo — means that a child who creates a MeetMe profile may receive messages and interactions from users of those platforms too. The effective user pool a child is exposed to is therefore significantly larger than MeetMe's own community alone. Parents should be aware that restricting one app may not be sufficient if linked apps in the same ecosystem remain accessible.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
MeetMe Allows Open Communication With Strangers at Scale
With over 100 million registered users and connections to a broader app ecosystem, MeetMe exposes a child to an exceptionally large community of unknown adults the moment they create a profile. There is no gatekeeping between adult users and children — open profile browsing, location-based discovery and direct messaging all allow unrestricted adult-to-child contact.
2
The Cross-Platform Ecosystem Is a Unique Risk Factor
MeetMe's integration with other Meet Group apps means that restricting MeetMe alone may not be sufficient if linked apps remain accessible on the same device. Parents should check whether apps including Skout, Lovoo or Zoosk are also installed — and apply consistent restrictions across all platforms within the same ecosystem to close this exposure gap.
3
Children May Install It Out of Curiosity or Social Influence
MeetMe's casual, social-first branding can make it appear to be a general social platform rather than a stranger-contact environment — particularly to younger users who encounter it through peers. The live streaming feature may appeal to children interested in content creation or viewing, without them recognising that the platform lacks the safety provisions of purpose-built creator platforms.
4
Age Verification Is Trivially Easy to Bypass
MeetMe requires only a date of birth to register. Entering a false birthdate gives a child immediate, unrestricted access to the full platform — including profile discovery across 100 million users, proximity-based nearby browsing, live video streams and direct messaging from unknown adults across multiple linked apps.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child from the specific risks of large-scale social discovery platforms.

💬
Teach Children Not to Communicate With Strangers Online
Have clear conversations about why platforms like MeetMe — where any of over 100 million unknown users can contact them and where live streams are largely unmoderated — are not safe environments for younger users. Help children understand the difference between a known-contact platform like a class group chat and an open-discovery platform where anyone can reach them. MeetMe's scale makes the stranger-contact risk concrete and tangible — use that context when talking with older children.
🧭
Discuss Privacy and Responsible Digital Behaviour
Talk with your child about what responsible digital behaviour means on platforms where strangers can see their profile and location and message them directly. Establish clear family expectations about what kinds of platforms are safe to use and why — framing it not as a restriction but as a conversation about which digital environments are genuinely built for their age group and which ones are not. Check all devices for linked apps from the same ecosystem, including Skout and Lovoo.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Prevent Access to Risky Chat Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses — including tablets, spare phones and shared family devices. Because MeetMe is part of a wider app ecosystem, also check for and restrict connected apps from The Meet Group. Review in-app purchase settings and stored payment methods to prevent diamond purchases. Combine device-level controls with regular family conversations about online safety for the most effective protection.
