Its regional focus means children using the platform are exposed to a very large adult user base spanning multiple countries and cultures.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Dating Apps Are Generally Intended for Adults 18 and Older
Paktor's terms of service, feature design and community culture are built for adult social and dating life across the Asia-Pacific region. The platform makes no provision for younger users and offers no child-safe content tier or experience.
2
Group Chats Add a Unique Exposure Layer
Unlike most dating apps that focus on one-to-one messaging, Paktor's group chat rooms expose children to adult-oriented discussions involving multiple unknown users simultaneously. This makes it harder to monitor or recognise concerning interactions than in a straightforward private conversation.
3
Curiosity and Peer Influence Drive Installation
Children and teenagers may install Paktor through peer recommendation or social media awareness, particularly in communities where the app is well known. The platform's social features and friendly interface can make it feel like a harmless social app — but it is built entirely for adult interaction.
4
Age Verification Is Simple to Bypass
Paktor requires only a date of birth and phone number or social account to register. A child can create a complete adult account in under a minute by entering a false birthdate. Review saved payment methods on shared devices to prevent in-app purchases from going unnoticed.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting digital awareness.

💬
Discuss Online Relationships and Digital Safety With Children
Have open, age-appropriate conversations about how dating apps like Paktor work, why they exist for adults, and what respectful online interaction looks like. Help children understand that group chats and private messaging with unknown adults carry risks that the friendly interface does not make visible — even when the people on the other side may seem harmless.
🤝
Encourage Communication Within Trusted Social Circles
Help your child invest in and build on the friendships and family relationships they already have, rather than seeking connection with unknown adults through dating platforms. Real-world relationships with trusted people provide a far stronger foundation than anonymous digital connections.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Limit Access to Adult-Focused Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses. Review in-app purchase settings and saved payment methods on shared devices to prevent unauthorised transactions. Combine controls with regular, honest family conversations — both together provide your most complete layer of protection.
