Designed for devices with limited storage or older hardware, Dual Space Lite reduces the app's installation footprint while preserving its core capability: enabling a child to maintain fully hidden secondary accounts across any platform they choose to clone.

Clone Any App Into a Fully Isolated Second Space
Dual Space Lite creates a completely separate virtual environment on the device where any cloned app runs with its own independent login, contacts, message history and activity. The second space shares no data with the primary environment — a parent inspecting the main space's apps, account settings or notification history finds no trace of what is running in the Lite environment alongside it.
📲
Separate Accounts on Every Cloned Platform
Each app cloned into Dual Space Lite's environment runs with a fully independent account — its own credentials, contacts and conversation history — entirely disconnected from the original. A child can maintain a completely different social media, messaging or gaming identity in the Lite environment, with no shared data, no common contacts and no notification overlap with the accounts a parent is aware of.
📦
Optimised for Low Storage — Works Well on Older and Secondary Devices
Dual Space Lite is specifically designed to run efficiently on devices with limited storage or older hardware — including hand-me-down phones, budget Android devices and secondary devices that a child may use alongside a primary one. Its reduced footprint means it can be installed and used on devices that cannot support a full-sized cloning tool, extending the reach of app cloning capability to a wider range of hardware a child might have access to.
🔔
Independent Notification Management Per Space
Like the full version, Dual Space Lite manages notifications for cloned apps independently from the primary space. Incoming messages and activity alerts from cloned accounts do not appear in the standard notification bar or on the lock screen alongside the primary space's notifications. A parent monitoring notification activity sees only the primary environment — the Lite space's notification stream is entirely separate.
🔒
No Cross-Space Data Sharing by Design
Dual Space Lite enforces the same strict separation between the primary space and the second space as the full version — no contacts, messages, media or account information crosses the boundary between the two environments. Examining the primary space provides no information about what is in the Lite environment. The only way to see the Lite space's contents is to open the app and navigate its cloned environment directly.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
App Cloning Tools Allow Children to Run a Hidden Second Online Presence on the Same Device
Dual Space Lite, like the full-sized cloning tools in the same category, is marketed for adults managing multiple professional or personal accounts. For children, the practical use case is almost always to maintain a secondary online presence alongside the primary one that parents see — with the Lite environment providing a structurally separate space where accounts, conversations and activity are invisible from the primary environment. The reduced feature set does not change this core dynamic.
2
Secondary Devices Are Often Outside Normal Oversight — and Dual Space Lite Works on Them
Many families maintain oversight of a child's primary device through regular check-ins, account visibility and parental control tools applied to their main phone. Secondary devices — older phones passed down within the family, budget Android devices used for games, tablets, or borrowed devices — are often subject to less systematic oversight. Dual Space Lite's low-storage optimisation makes it specifically suitable for these lower-specification devices, meaning it may be operating on hardware that a parent is not actively monitoring at the same level as a primary device.
3
Children May Choose the Lite Version Specifically Because It Leaves a Smaller Trace
A child who is aware of both versions of Dual Space may deliberately choose the Lite version because it installs faster, uses less visible storage, and draws less attention from a parent who may notice a large new app download on the device. The Lite version's discretion is a practical advantage for a child trying to install a cloning tool without it being noticed — meaning its presence on a device should be taken just as seriously as the full-sized version.
4
The Second Space Cannot Be Seen From the Primary Space by Design
Standard device review approaches — examining installed app accounts, checking notification history, browsing app settings or reviewing message threads — will not reveal the contents of Dual Space Lite's second environment. The boundary between the two spaces is enforced by the app's architecture. A parent who has recently reviewed the primary environment may have an accurate picture of that space while the Lite environment operates entirely undiscovered on the same device. Category-level blocking before installation is the only reliably effective approach.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to address app cloning tools on your child's devices — including older and secondary hardware — and build lasting habits of honest, single-identity online behaviour.

💬
Encourage Open Discussions About Responsible Online Behaviour
If you find Dual Space Lite on any of your child's devices, begin with a calm and open-ended conversation. Ask what was cloned, which accounts were running in the second space, and what prompted the decision to use a separate hidden environment. Finding the Lite version specifically — rather than the full edition — may indicate that a child has made a deliberate choice around discretion: they chose the smaller, harder-to-notice version. Approach that with curiosity about what they needed privacy for, rather than leading immediately with the fact that it is restricted. Understanding the motivation — platform, contacts, content, or simply a desire for more space from parental visibility — gives you a much better foundation for a productive and lasting conversation about trust, safety and online identity.
🧭
Explain the Risks of Maintaining Hidden or Secondary Accounts
Help your child understand that the risks of a hidden account in Dual Space Lite's second environment are not limited to what parents can see — they are risks to the child themselves. An account operating outside a child's normal social environment typically involves less caution, different contacts, and weaker personal safeguards than the primary accounts they manage carefully. Without familiar social anchors — the friends, peers and shared context of their regular online environment — children in hidden account spaces are more exposed to manipulation and are less likely to apply the protective judgement they use in visible settings. A hidden account is not just private from parents; it is also a context where a child's own protective instincts tend to be weaker.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to App Cloning Tools
Apply iLimits restrictions across every device your child uses — not just their primary phone. Dual Space Lite's low-storage optimisation makes it well suited to older, secondary and hand-me-down devices that may not be part of a family's primary oversight routine. A consistent restriction policy that covers every device removes the secondary-device loophole entirely. Category-level blocking prevents Dual Space Lite and all similar tools from being installed before a hidden environment can be established — without requiring you to know the app's name or identify a small, discreet install in a storage report. Pair this with regular honest conversations about which accounts and platforms your child uses across all their devices, so your oversight covers the full picture of their online life rather than just the device you see most often.
