💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child from adult entertainment content and build a foundation of open, honest conversation about responsible internet use and age-appropriate digital habits.

💬
Talk Openly With Children About Responsible Internet Use
Age-appropriate, honest conversation about adult content — what it is, why it exists and why it is not suitable for children — is one of the most effective protective tools available to parents. Children who receive a clear and honest explanation of why certain platforms are restricted, rather than simply being told they are off-limits without explanation, are better equipped to make the right decision when they encounter adult content in an uncontrolled environment. The conversation does not need to be extensive or clinical — it needs to be calm, honest and framed around the child's wellbeing rather than punishment or shame. Equally important is ensuring that children know they can come to a parent without fear if they encounter something online that concerns or confuses them.
🌱
Encourage Healthy and Age-Appropriate Digital Habits
Help your child develop a thoughtful, balanced approach to the internet by establishing household norms around the kinds of content that are and are not appropriate at their current age, and by making it normal to discuss what they are seeing online rather than treating it as a private or hidden activity. Children who feel that their digital life is something they can share with a parent — rather than something they need to conceal — are significantly more resilient to harmful content encounters. Reinforce that restrictions on adult content platforms are not punishments but protections, in the same way that age ratings on films, television and games protect children from content that is not appropriate for their stage of development.
🔒
Use Parental Control Tools to Restrict Access to Adult Content Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child has access to — tablets, secondary phones and shared family computers included — and review those restrictions periodically as children acquire new devices or access new networks. Because adult entertainment platforms are accessible through browsers without any installation, consider enabling safe-search settings on all browsers used by children as an additional layer of protection. Also review the payment methods stored on shared devices to prevent a subscription service from being accessed using saved payment credentials. Technical controls and open conversation together form the most complete and durable protection available to your family.
