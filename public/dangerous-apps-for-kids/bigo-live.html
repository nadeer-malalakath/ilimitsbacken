What This App Does

A clear picture of BIGO Live — a live streaming platform where users broadcast themselves in real time to a global audience of strangers, and where viewers interact directly with broadcasters through public and private messaging.

📡
BIGO Live Is Built Around Real-Time Video Broadcasting to an Unlimited Audience
BIGO Live's core design puts live video broadcasting at the centre of every user's experience — anyone can go live instantly and reach viewers from any country, with no meaningful gate between opening the app and broadcasting to or viewing a global audience. This always-on, real-time environment removes every layer of delay or review that conventional social media provides, exposing children to unscreened adult content and interactions the moment the app is opened.

Multi-Guest Streams
BIGO Live is a live streaming and social video platform where users can broadcast live video and interact with viewers in real time. Users can watch live streams, send messages and communicate with broadcasters or other viewers from around the world. The app focuses on social entertainment and real-time online interaction — all of which occurs without content pre-screening or parental visibility.
🎥
Instant Live Video Broadcasting to a Global Audience
Any user can begin broadcasting live video to a worldwide audience in seconds — with no content review, no qualification required and no moderation buffer before the stream goes public. A child can begin broadcasting their face, location and daily environment to an unlimited number of unknown adults around the world from the moment the app is installed.
💬
Open Live Chat Between Viewers and Broadcasters
During any live stream, all viewers can send public messages to the broadcaster in real time. These chats are unmoderated, contain no content filtering and are visible to every other viewer in the stream — meaning children watching a stream may see inappropriate messages from other adult users, and children broadcasting are exposed to anything an adult viewer chooses to write to them directly.
🎁
Virtual Gift System That Incentivises Ongoing Interaction
BIGO Live features a virtual gift economy where viewers send digital gifts to broadcasters during live streams — gifts that can be converted into real-world monetary value. This creates a commercial incentive for broadcasters to please their audience and encourages viewers to spend real money on the platform. Children can be drawn into this system on either side, spending money or feeling pressure to perform for an audience that rewards them.
✉️
Private Messaging With Unknown Users
Beyond public live chat, BIGO Live allows users to send and receive private direct messages from anyone on the platform. These private conversations are completely invisible to parents, unmonitored and contain no content filtering — creating a private channel between a child and any unknown adult who chooses to make contact after watching them on a live stream.
🔓
No Effective Age Verification
BIGO Live requires only a self-reported age at registration with no verification of the information provided. A child gains full access to live broadcasting, global viewer interaction, private messaging and the virtual gift economy within moments of installing the app. Adults can equally misrepresent their age or identity with nothing in place to prevent it.

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from exposure to unscreened live content, real-time interaction with unknown adults, financial mechanics designed for adults and significant privacy concerns from live video broadcasting.

🔞
Mature or Inappropriate Live Streams
Live content is not reviewed before it goes public — children can encounter adult-only, sexual or disturbing material in real time with no content warning and no moderation delay
👁️
Unknown Adults Watching Children Broadcast
A child who broadcasts live is immediately visible to a global adult audience — including individuals whose intentions cannot be verified and who can interact with the child directly during the stream
💬
Inappropriate Live Chat Conversations
Live chat during broadcasts is unmoderated and can include sexually explicit, harassing or otherwise inappropriate messages from adult viewers that children see in real time
🔒
Privacy Risks From Live Broadcasting
Children broadcasting live may inadvertently reveal their face, home environment, school, location or daily routine to an unknown global audience — information that cannot be recalled once a stream has ended
💸
Virtual Gift and Monetisation Mechanics
The gift economy creates financial risk — children may spend real money sending gifts, or be placed under social and emotional pressure to continue broadcasting to maintain audience approval and incoming gifts
😟
Grooming via Private Messages
Adults who watch a child's live stream can send private messages afterwards — creating an unmonitored, private channel that is invisible to parents and that children may not recognise as potentially unsafe
🟠

Live Streaming Is Not Like Pre-Recorded Video — Content Cannot Be Reviewed Before It Is Seen
Unlike on-demand video platforms where content is reviewed and categorised before it reaches viewers, live streaming on BIGO Live is entirely real-time and unscreened. A child viewing a stream or broadcasting themselves is in an environment where anything can happen at any moment — and where parental intervention after the fact is not possible because no record of what occurred is retained or accessible.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Live Streaming Platforms Represent a Fundamentally Different Risk to Pre-Recorded Video
Parents who are comfortable with their child watching YouTube or Netflix may not immediately recognise that live streaming involves an entirely different category of risk. On BIGO Live, every stream is happening in real time with no prior review — the audience is interactive, the content is unpredictable and the broadcaster is live in front of an unscreened global audience. A child can encounter adult content or be approached by an unknown adult before any moderation or intervention is possible.
2
Children Broadcasting Live Are Immediately Visible to a Worldwide Adult Audience
When a child goes live on BIGO Live, they are instantly broadcasting their face, voice and surroundings to every user on the platform who discovers their stream — with no restriction on who can watch. Adults who watch a child broadcast can interact with them in the live chat, follow their account and send private messages after the stream ends. The child has no reliable way of knowing who is watching them or what their intentions are.
3
The Gift Economy Can Create Unhealthy Pressure and Financial Risk
BIGO Live's virtual gift system is designed to drive engagement and spending. Children who broadcast may develop emotional attachment to the validation that comes from receiving gifts — and feel pressure to continue streaming, change their behaviour or keep their audience happy in order to maintain that approval. Children viewing streams may spend real money sending gifts without fully understanding the financial implications. Neither dynamic is appropriate for younger users.
4
Private Messages From Strangers Are Invisible to Parents by Default
Any adult who watches a child's live stream can follow up with a private direct message — opening an unmonitored conversation channel that parents cannot access. Unlike social media platforms where a child typically only receives messages from people they follow back, BIGO Live's structure means that anyone who discovers a child's stream can immediately attempt private contact. There is no parental visibility into these messages and no notification if an adult makes contact.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around responsible live streaming use, privacy and safe digital behaviour.

🎙️
Talk With Children About Responsible Use of Live Streaming Platforms
Help your child understand that going live on an app means broadcasting to an unknown global audience in real time — not just sharing a video with friends. Discuss specifically what should never appear in a live stream: their face in combination with personal details, their home address or school name, their daily routine, and any information that could be used to identify or locate them. Explain that unlike a social media post, a live stream cannot be taken back — anything seen by viewers during the broadcast has already been seen and cannot be removed.
🛡️
Encourage Safe Online Behaviour and Privacy Awareness
Talk regularly with your child about online safety in the context of live video — what it means for a stranger to be watching them in real time, why private messages from people they do not know should be treated with caution, and what to do if a live chat or private message makes them feel uncomfortable. Children who understand the specific risks of live streaming — rather than treating it as equivalent to watching pre-recorded video — are significantly better equipped to make safe decisions independently.
🔒
Use Parental Control Tools to Manage Access to Live Streaming Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child has access to — including tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices. Live streaming apps are frequently reinstalled within minutes of being removed and are often discovered through peer recommendations, making device-level controls significantly more reliable than app-by-app management. Combine technical restrictions with ongoing, open conversations about digital safety to build the most complete and sustainable layer of protection available for your family.
🔐

Safety Online Starts With the Conversations You Have at Home

No parental control tool can replace the protection that comes from a child who understands why certain platforms are unsafe and feels comfortable talking to their parents about what they encounter online. iLimits gives you the technical layer — the conversations, trust and awareness your family builds together provide the rest.
