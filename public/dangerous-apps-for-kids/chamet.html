Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from live video with strangers, exposure to adult content, unpredictable interactions and significant privacy and safety concerns unique to real-time anonymous contact.

📹
Live Video With Strangers
Children are instantly connected via real-time two-way video with a random adult from anywhere in the world — with no preview, no opt-in and no knowledge of who will appear before the session is already live
🔞
Inappropriate or Adult Content
Random live video with an unfiltered global adult user base means children can be exposed to explicit, adult or disturbing behaviour in real time with no content warning and no moderation buffer between the match and the exposure
⚡
Unpredictable Interactions
Because matching is random and interactions are live, the nature of any given conversation is entirely unpredictable — children have no way to anticipate what a stranger will say or do before they are already connected via live video
🔒
Privacy and Safety Concerns
Children may share personal information — their face, name, school or location — with unknown adults during video calls or private messages, with no oversight and no verification of who receives that information or how it may be used
😰
Harassment and Unsafe Contact
The live, anonymous and real-time nature of Chamet creates significant risk of direct harassment, manipulation or threatening behaviour, with no effective protective mechanism during an active video or chat session
🔗
Ongoing Stranger Relationships
Follow and messaging features allow live encounters to become persistent, private contact between a child and an unknown adult — well beyond any isolated video match and entirely hidden from parents

Real-Time and Often Anonymous — There Is No Window to Intervene
Live video chat platforms connect children with strangers instantly and in real time. Because these interactions happen live and often anonymously, children may encounter inappropriate conversations, unsafe individuals or content that is not suitable for their age before any opportunity to disengage — and long before a parent is aware that an interaction has taken place. 👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Chamet Combines the Two Highest-Risk Features Into One Platform
By layering social discovery on top of live random video, Chamet creates a risk environment more complex than either feature in isolation. Children are not only exposed to unpredictable live video with strangers — they can also be found, profiled and followed by adults who have seen them in a match. This combination of immediate exposure and ongoing contact potential makes Chamet among the highest-risk platforms available to younger users.
2
Interactions Are Live, Real-Time and Leave No Recoverable Record
Live video sessions on Chamet produce no log that parents can review. A child can have a distressing or harmful interaction, close the app and say nothing — with nothing recorded, no alert sent and no trace for a parent to find. This absence of any recoverable record makes Chamet exceptionally difficult to monitor through conventional means, and underscores why device-level controls are more reliable than retrospective review for this category of app.
3
Children May Not Perceive the Risk Until Exposure Has Already Occurred
Chamet's clean, social interface does not signal its risks visibly at the point of installation or first use. A child may open the app with curiosity and be connected live to an unknown adult before they have formed any expectation of what that match might involve. The real-time nature of the platform means there is no preview, no buffer and no moment of reflection between tapping to match and being face-to-face with a stranger on screen.
4
Social Discovery Features Create Risk That Outlasts Any Single Session
Unlike purely random video apps, Chamet's follow and private messaging features mean that risk does not end when a video session closes. An adult who encountered a child through a live match can follow their profile, send messages and request further video contact — creating an ongoing dynamic that parents may have no awareness of for an extended period of time. The cumulative risk of sustained contact with an unknown adult is significantly greater than that of a single random encounter.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around live stranger contact and safe digital behaviour.

💬
Teach Children About the Risks of Interacting With Strangers Online
Have a calm, direct conversation with your child about why live video with strangers is unsafe — not as an abstract rule but as something they genuinely understand. Explain that a friendly or relatable person on a live video call cannot be verified, that conversations can escalate quickly and without warning, and that the real-time nature of the platform means there is no opportunity to review or undo what happens once a session has begun. Establish that if they ever encounter something unsettling on any platform, they can come to you without fear of judgement or punishment — children who feel safe disclosing are significantly better protected than those who stay silent.
🤝
Encourage Open Conversations About Online Safety
Online safety is most effective when it is an ongoing conversation rather than a single warning. Ask your child regularly about what they are using, who they are speaking to and what they enjoy about those platforms. Discuss why live video with strangers carries risks that conventional social media does not, and help them identify safer ways to meet the same social needs — school activities, existing friend groups, interest communities with known members. Children who feel genuinely heard and connected in their real-world social life are less likely to seek connection through open-access stranger platforms.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Restrict Access to Anonymous Video Chat Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses — tablets, secondary phones and shared household devices included. Apps like Chamet are frequently reinstalled within minutes of being deleted, making device-level controls significantly more reliable than manual app removal. Combine technical restrictions with regular, honest conversations about online safety for the most complete and durable layer of protection your family can build together.
