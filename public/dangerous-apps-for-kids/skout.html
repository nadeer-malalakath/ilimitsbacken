📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear picture of Skout's global stranger-chat design, its history of child safety incidents and why it remains a concern for parents today.

📋
Skout Has a Documented History of Child Safety Incidents
Skout is one of the earlier location-based stranger-chat platforms, launched in 2007. In 2012 the company temporarily suspended its teen section following multiple reported sexual assaults on minors who had connected with adults through the app. While safeguards have been updated since, Skout remains a platform where adults and strangers can freely interact — with no meaningful identity verification and minimal content moderation in place.

Skout is a global social discovery and stranger-chat platform that allows users to meet and chat with new people nearby or around the world. Users create profiles, browse others by location, participate in live broadcasts and send messages — all within an open environment where no prior connection or mutual consent is required to make contact.
📍
Location-Based Nearby Discovery
Skout's core feature surfaces other users based on their physical proximity. A child using the platform is immediately visible to unknown adults nearby — with no opt-out from local discovery available without fully disabling location access on the device.
🤝
Shake to Chat — Random Contact
Skout's "Shake to Chat" feature connects users with a random stranger on the platform. This removes even the limited gatekeeping of profile browsing — a child shaking their device is immediately connected with an unknown adult for unmoderated real-time chat.
📺
Live Broadcasting
Users can broadcast live video to the Skout community and receive real-time comments and virtual gifts. Live streams are largely unmoderated and expose viewers — including children — to adult behaviour and conversations without warning or filtering.
💬
Open & Unmoderated Private Messaging
Any user can send a message to any other user on the platform. Private chats are unscreened and entirely private — there is no content moderation, no parental visibility and no two-way consent requirement before contact begins.
🎁
Points, Gifts & In-App Purchases
Skout uses a virtual points economy for sending gifts and boosting profiles. Points are purchased with real money through in-app payments. Children with access to a parent's device or saved payment method could make purchases without parental awareness.
🌐
Global Reach With No Identity Verification
Skout's user base spans the globe with millions of registered users across many countries. Registration requires only a date of birth — no identity check confirms that users are the age they claim, meaning adults can easily present as peers to children on the platform.
🔞
Skout Is Intended for Users 17 or Older The platform's open stranger-contact model, live broadcasting and unmoderated private messaging are not appropriate for children at any age. No child-safe mode or age-restricted content tier is available.
🎲
"Shake to Chat" Randomly Connects Children With Unknown Adults
Skout's Shake to Chat feature is unique — and uniquely risky. Unlike profile browsing where a child at least chooses who they engage with, Shake to Chat removes all choice: a single gesture connects a child instantly and randomly with any adult on the platform for unmoderated real-time chat. This is one of the most indiscriminate stranger-contact mechanisms available on any mainstream social platform.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Skout Allows Open Interaction Between Strangers — Including Adults and Children
Skout's design prioritises open discovery and contact between strangers over user safety. There is no meaningful gatekeeping between adult users and any child who creates a profile — location-based discovery, random Shake to Chat and open messaging all allow adults to initiate contact with children without restriction.
2
Skout's History Provides Important Context for Parents
Skout is one of the few mainstream social platforms with a publicly documented history of child safety failures, including the 2012 suspension of its teen section following reported assaults. While the company has updated its policies since, the platform's fundamental open-contact model — which made those incidents possible — remains largely unchanged. Parents should weigh this history when assessing the risk Skout presents.
3
Children May Install It Out of Curiosity or Peer Influence
Skout's playful interface, discovery features and Shake to Chat mechanic can appeal to younger users looking for new social experiences. Children may encounter it through peers or social media and install it without understanding that it is an adult-facing platform with no meaningful child safety provisions.
4
Age Checks Are Trivially Easy to Bypass
Skout requires only a date of birth to register. A child entering a false birthdate gains immediate, unrestricted access to the full platform — including location-based discovery, Shake to Chat with random strangers, live broadcasts and unmoderated private messaging with any adult user on the platform.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build responsible digital habits around stranger-chat platforms.

💬
Teach Children Not to Communicate With Strangers Online
Have clear, age-appropriate conversations about why platforms like Skout — where any adult can contact them and random chat with strangers is a built-in feature — are not safe environments. Help children understand that online strangers are genuinely unknown, that adults can misrepresent themselves as peers, and that a friendly interaction at first contact does not mean the other person is safe. Skout's history of documented incidents can be a useful, factual starting point for this conversation with older children.
🧭
Discuss Privacy and Responsible Digital Behaviour
Talk with your child about the information they share online and what responsible digital behaviour looks like — especially on platforms where location is a primary feature. Help them understand that sharing their approximate location with unknown adults is a concrete physical risk, not just a digital one. Establish clear family expectations about which platforms are safe to use, why, and what to do if something feels wrong in an online interaction.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Prevent Access to Risky Chat Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses. Stranger-chat apps like Skout are easily reinstalled after deletion, so device-level category blocking provides more reliable protection than manually checking for specific app installs. Review device location permissions and in-app purchase settings regularly, and keep honest family conversations about online safety running alongside your parental controls — the two work best together.
