👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Sports Events Drive Curiosity
During major tournaments like the IPL, World Cup or Premier League, betting apps are heavily advertised. Children who watch sports with adults are frequently exposed to this messaging and may become curious about how it works.
2
Age Verification Is Easy to Bypass
Most gambling platforms require users to be 18+, but these checks can be bypassed using a parent's details, a copied ID, or by simply lying about their age during sign-up.
3
Talk About Probability and Money
Open conversations about how odds work, why "the house always wins," and the value of money earned through effort are among the most effective protective factors parents can offer.
4
Watch for Behavioural Changes
Secretive phone use, asking for money often, or emotional reactions tied to sports outcomes can all be early signs that a child has started using betting or gambling apps.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps you can take right now.

💬
Talk Openly About Gambling Risks
Explain in age-appropriate terms how gambling works, why it's designed for people to lose, and why it's not suitable for children. A calm, non-judgmental conversation is far more powerful than silent restriction alone.
♟️
Encourage Skill-Based Games Instead
Redirect interest toward chess, coding challenges, quizzes, or real-world sports — activities where outcomes are driven by genuine skill and effort, not chance or money.
🔒
Use Parental Controls Consistently
Restrictions work best when applied consistently across all devices. Review your iLimits settings regularly as new apps emerge and your child's digital environment evolves.
