📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear look at how Vaulty works and why its design creates specific concerns for families with children.

Vaulty is a mobile application designed to hide photos and videos inside a secure, password-protected vault — keeping that content completely invisible to anyone who does not hold the key.
🖼️
Moves Media Into a Hidden Vault
Users can transfer photos and videos directly from the device's normal gallery into Vaulty's protected storage area. Once moved, the original files are removed from the gallery entirely.
🔐
Password, PIN or Biometric Lock
The vault is secured with a personal password, PIN code or fingerprint — making all content inside completely inaccessible to anyone without the correct credentials.
👁️‍🗨️
Invisible to the Standard Gallery
Files stored inside Vaulty do not appear in the device's default photo gallery or file manager — making them entirely hidden during routine checks of the device.
🤳
Captures Media Directly Inside the App
Vaulty includes a built-in camera that saves photos and videos directly to the vault, bypassing the standard gallery entirely so content is hidden from the moment it is created.
📥
Imports From Any Source
Media can be imported into the vault from messaging apps, social platforms or any other source on the device — consolidating hidden content from multiple locations into one locked space.
⚠️ Section 2

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Specific concerns that arise when children use hidden vault apps on their personal devices.

👁️
Hidden From Parents
Vault apps are explicitly built to hide content — making it very difficult for parents to know what media their child is storing on their device
🚫
Bypasses Supervision
Standard gallery checks become ineffective the moment content is concealed inside a locked vault that parents cannot access
📵
Inappropriate Content Storage
Children may use the vault to store explicit or age-inappropriate images and videos behind a password that adults cannot easily detect or reach
📉
Reduced Transparency
Vault apps fundamentally reduce the open, transparent device environment that is essential for keeping children safe online
🤳
Secret In-App Camera
The built-in camera captures media that goes directly into the vault — bypassing the gallery and leaving no trace of what was photographed or recorded
🔗
Enables Secret Sharing
Children may use the vault to store images received privately from unknown contacts — with no visibility for the adults responsible for their safety
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Vault Apps Are Purpose-Built to Hide Content
Unlike general privacy settings, Vaulty is specifically designed with the goal of concealing media from other people. This is not a secondary feature — it is the entire purpose of the app, making oversight by parents structurally difficult.
2
Children May Use It Without Harmful Intent
Not every child who installs a vault app is hiding something harmful. Some are simply curious or seeking a sense of personal space. However, the hidden nature of the app itself removes an important layer of parental visibility regardless of the child's intent.
3
Normal Gallery Checks Will Not Reveal Hidden Content
If a child is using Vaulty, reviewing the standard photo gallery will show an incomplete picture of the device's content. Parents who rely on casual device checks may have a false sense of awareness about what their child has stored.
4
Healthy Privacy Is Different From Hidden Secrecy
Children's desire for personal space is natural and healthy. The concern is not the impulse for privacy itself — it is the ability to completely conceal content from the adults who are responsible for their wellbeing. The goal is transparency, not surveillance.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to help your child develop open, healthy digital habits.

💬
Encourage Open Conversations About Digital Habits
Talk regularly and openly with your child about what they keep on their device and why. Children who feel safe to share with a trusted adult are far less likely to seek hidden spaces. Curiosity and the desire for privacy are normal — secrecy from caregivers is where the risk lies.
📱
Discuss Responsible and Transparent Device Use
Help your child understand the difference between healthy personal space and hiding things from the people who care for them. Set clear, age-appropriate expectations: what types of content should always be visible to a trusted adult, and why that openness keeps them safer.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Restrict Hidden Storage Apps
Technology-based safeguards work best when paired with honest, ongoing dialogue. Use iLimits to block vault-style apps across every device your child uses — and reinforce those controls with regular conversations about why digital transparency matters for their safety and wellbeing.
