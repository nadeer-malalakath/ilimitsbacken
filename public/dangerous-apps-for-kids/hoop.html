📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear picture of Hoop — a social discovery app that connects users with strangers by funnelling new contacts directly into private external messaging platforms where parental oversight is significantly reduced.

🔗
Hoop Is a Pipeline to Private Stranger Contact on External Platforms
Unlike standalone chat apps, Hoop's primary function is to connect users with strangers and route those connections into external messaging platforms — most commonly Snapchat. This two-stage design means that by the time a conversation begins, it has moved entirely outside any moderation Hoop itself provides, into a private channel where parents have no visibility and no way to monitor what is being exchanged.
👻
Hoop Actively Routes Children Into Private Snapchat Conversations With Strangers
Hoop is specifically designed around Snapchat integration — users share Snapchat usernames on their profiles and add strangers directly. Once a connection moves to Snapchat, the conversation is entirely private, self-deleting and invisible to parents. The stranger relationship that began on Hoop is now beyond the reach of any monitoring tool within Hoop itself.

Hoop is a social discovery application designed to help users meet new people online. The app allows users to discover profiles and connect with others — often linking users directly to external messaging platforms such as Snapchat. Users can quickly add and interact with people they have never met before, with the primary goal of building new contacts outside their existing social circle. The pathway from stranger to private contact is rapid, by design, and moves beyond any oversight the app itself provides.
👆
Swipe-Based Stranger Discovery
Hoop uses swipe mechanics to browse and express interest in stranger profiles — borrowed directly from dating app design. The familiarity of the swipe format makes contact-seeking feel casual and low-stakes to children, reducing their awareness of the risk involved in adding people they have never previously encountered.
👻
Snapchat Integration as the Primary Contact Route
Hoop's core mechanic is the exchange of Snapchat usernames between matched strangers. Users display their Snapchat handle on their Hoop profile, and connections are completed by adding each other on Snapchat. Once contact moves there, Hoop's environment is bypassed entirely — conversations become private, ephemeral and invisible to any parent monitoring the Hoop app itself.
🌍
Global Profile Browsing With No Geographic Filter
Hoop allows users to browse profiles of strangers from anywhere in the world without any geographic or demographic safety filter designed to protect younger users. A child is not limited to potential contacts of a similar age or location — they are exposed to an unfiltered global pool of users whose ages, identities and intentions are entirely unverified.
📇
Rapid Add Mechanics That Minimise Hesitation
Hoop is designed to make adding strangers as quick and frictionless as possible. The speed and simplicity of the add process is a deliberate design choice — it reduces the moment of reflection between a child seeing a profile and exchanging contact details with that person, lowering the natural caution they might otherwise apply.
🔓
No Meaningful Age or Identity Verification
Hoop requires only a self-reported date of birth at registration with no verification of any kind. Adults can present as peers of any age, and children have no reliable method of confirming who they are adding before the contact is already established and has moved to a private external platform.

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from stranger connections routed to private external platforms, inappropriate conversations, contact detail exposure and the fundamental difficulty of monitoring what happens after a Hoop connection is made.

👤
Interaction With Strangers Online
Hoop's sole purpose is connecting users with people they do not know — children are encouraged to browse, swipe and add strangers from a global pool with no meaningful vetting of who those people are
👻
Private External Platform Contact
By routing connections to Snapchat, Hoop moves stranger contact into a private, self-deleting messaging environment — entirely outside parental view and beyond the reach of any oversight applied to the Hoop app itself
💬
Inappropriate Conversations
Once a connection leaves Hoop and enters a private platform, the content of any conversation is entirely unmonitored — children may encounter inappropriate, adult or harmful messaging with no content screening or record
📇
Personal Contact Detail Exposure
Adding a stranger on Snapchat via Hoop involves sharing a personal contact handle — and may extend to phone numbers, real names or location — with an unknown adult whose identity and intentions are completely unverified
🎭
Age Misrepresentation
Adults can easily create Hoop profiles presenting as teenagers — children browsing discovery profiles have no way to verify the stated age of a stranger before adding them and initiating private contact on an external platform
🕳️
Parental Supervision Blind Spot
Because the most significant risk occurs after a child leaves Hoop for Snapchat, monitoring the Hoop app alone is insufficient — parents may have no awareness that private stranger contact is already ongoing on a separate platform
🟠

Hoop Creates a Parental Supervision Blind Spot by Design
Social discovery apps are designed to help users meet new people quickly — and Hoop specifically moves those new contacts into private messaging platforms where parental supervision becomes significantly more difficult. Children may connect with strangers on Hoop and continue those conversations on Snapchat, entirely outside a parent's view. Monitoring the Hoop app in isolation does not reveal what is happening in the private conversations it has created.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Hoop's Risk Does Not End When the App Is Closed
Because Hoop's primary function is to route stranger contacts to external platforms, the risk it creates persists well beyond any use of the Hoop app itself. A child who adds a stranger on Hoop may be exchanging private messages with that person on Snapchat for days, weeks or months — and a parent who monitors only the Hoop app will have no visibility of those ongoing conversations. The discovery happens on Hoop; the harm can happen elsewhere.
2
Snapchat's Disappearing Messages Amplify the Risk
Hoop's tight integration with Snapchat is not coincidental — Snapchat's self-deleting messages make it significantly harder for parents to review conversations after the fact. By specifically directing new contacts to Snapchat, Hoop ensures that what passes between a child and a stranger leaves no lasting record that a parent could discover. The combination of Hoop's discovery mechanics and Snapchat's ephemeral messaging creates an unusually difficult environment for parental oversight.
3
Dating-App Mechanics in a Platform Accessible to Children
Hoop uses swipe-to-discover mechanics borrowed directly from adult dating applications. The familiarity of the format for teenagers — who may recognise the swipe gesture from other contexts — can normalise the process of adding unknown people as social contacts. Parents should be aware that a design inspired by adult dating apps is being used by children on a platform with no meaningful verification of who the other users are.
4
Children May Not Perceive Hoop as Risky — It Feels Like Normal Social Browsing
Hoop's interface is clean, social and designed to feel like casual profile browsing rather than contact with strangers. Children using it may perceive adding people through Hoop as broadly equivalent to following someone on Instagram — a low-stakes, social action rather than a risk. This perception gap is significant, and means children may not apply caution to Hoop that they would to a platform they more clearly associate with unknown-person contact.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around stranger connections, external platform risks and safe social networking.

💬
Talk With Children About the Risks of Connecting With Strangers Online
Have a direct, calm conversation with your child about what it means to add someone you have never met as a contact — not just on Hoop, but on the external platforms those connections lead to. Explain that a profile picture and a shared interest do not tell you who someone really is, and that moving a conversation to Snapchat with a stranger removes every layer of protection and leaves no record. Specifically discuss the Hoop-to-Snapchat pipeline: help them understand that what feels like casual profile browsing on one app is actually the first step towards a private conversation with an unknown adult on another. Make it genuinely safe for them to come to you if they have already added someone who has made them feel uncomfortable.
🤝
Encourage Safe Use of Social Networking Apps
Help your child develop a clear personal rule around social contacts: only add people they have met and know in real life. Talk through why this matters — not as an arbitrary restriction, but as something they genuinely understand. Discuss what responsible social networking looks like in practice, including what information should never be shared with a new contact, how to recognise when a stranger is pushing for more personal details than is appropriate, and how to end a conversation that feels uncomfortable without feeling pressure to continue it. Children who have internalised these principles are safer in every digital environment, not just on Hoop.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to Social Discovery Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses — tablets, secondary phones and any shared household devices included. Social discovery apps like Hoop are frequently reinstalled quickly after deletion, and may not be familiar enough by name for parents monitoring manually to recognise them immediately. Device-level category restrictions prevent reinstallation and are significantly more reliable than app-by-app removal. Where possible, also review and tighten privacy settings on external platforms your child already uses — including Snapchat — to limit who can add them as a contact from outside their existing network.
🧩

Safe Connections Are Made With People You Already Know

The strongest protection for your child online is a clear understanding of who they are actually talking to — and the confidence to come to you when something doesn't feel right. iLimits gives you the technical tools; the conversations you have together provide the foundation that no app can replace.
