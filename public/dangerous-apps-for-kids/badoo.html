👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Badoo Is Designed for Adults 18 and Older
Badoo's terms of service, feature design and user community are built entirely for adult social and dating life. The platform has no content tier or experience designed for younger users, and the adult norms, conversations and content throughout the app reflect this.
2
Live Video Adds a Unique Layer of Risk
Badoo's live streaming feature is particularly concerning because it enables real-time, unmoderated video interaction between a child and unknown adults. Unlike text messaging, live video creates immediate and immersive exposure to adult content and behaviour that cannot easily be undone.
3
Curiosity and Peer Influence Drive Installation
Children may encounter Badoo through peers, social media or general curiosity about meeting new people online. Once inside the platform, its scale and feature variety make it easy to become deeply engaged before a parent is aware the app has been installed.
4
Age Verification Is Straightforward to Bypass
Badoo requires only an email address, phone number and date of birth to register. A child can create a full account by entering a false birthdate in seconds. No additional identity verification is required before the complete platform becomes accessible.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting digital resilience.

💬
Discuss Online Relationships and Digital Safety
Have open, age-appropriate conversations about how social discovery apps work, why platforms like Badoo exist for adults, and what safe, respectful online interaction looks like. Pay special attention to live video risks — explain that real-time video with strangers can expose them to content and situations that are impossible to predict or reverse.
🤝
Encourage Interaction Within Trusted Social Circles
Help your child build and prioritise their existing relationships — with family, close friends and known peers — rather than seeking connection with hundreds of millions of unknown adults online. Strong real-world bonds make anonymous digital platforms far less appealing and necessary.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Prevent Access to Adult-Focused Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across all your child's devices. Also review in-app purchase settings and saved payment methods on shared devices to prevent accidental credit purchases. Revisit your settings regularly as your child grows — controls and conversations together provide the most complete protection.
