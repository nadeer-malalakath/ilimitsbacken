💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child from the financial and psychological risks of online gambling platforms and to build durable awareness around responsible gaming habits.

🎓
Teach Children About the Risks of Gambling-Related Games
Help your child understand in concrete, honest terms what gambling means and why it is an adult activity with genuine risks. Explain that every hand of poker on a real-money platform involves staking actual money on an outcome they cannot control — and that even highly skilled players lose money over time because the platform extracts a percentage of every pot as its fee. Discuss the psychological mechanics that make gambling difficult to stop once started: the excitement of anticipation, the urge to recover a loss with the next hand, and the way platforms are designed to make those feelings as strong as possible. Children who understand these mechanics specifically, rather than receiving a general warning, are significantly better equipped to recognise and resist them if they encounter a gambling platform.
🤝
Encourage Responsible Gaming Habits
Establish clear household principles around digital gaming that include an honest discussion of what makes certain games different from others: that games involving real money and uncertain outcomes carry risks that free games do not, and that those risks are specifically why they are restricted to adults. Help your child develop the habit of asking, before engaging with any new game, whether real money is involved and what happens when it is lost. Reinforce that enjoyment of sports and card games does not require financial stakes, and that the pleasure of competition is available through free-to-play alternatives without the financial and psychological risks that real-money platforms introduce.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Restrict Access to Gambling Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child can access — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Also review and secure the payment methods stored on shared devices: consider setting purchase authentication requirements that prevent any transaction without active parental approval. Online gambling platforms including poker apps are frequently discovered through advertising, peer discussion and social media promotion, making proactive device-level restrictions far more reliable than responding after a child has already downloaded the app and made their first deposit. The earlier a restriction is in place, the more effective it is.
