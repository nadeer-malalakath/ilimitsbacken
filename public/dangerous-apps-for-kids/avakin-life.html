What This App Does

A clear picture of Avakin Life — a persistent 3D virtual social world where users build customisable avatars, explore shared virtual spaces and socialise freely with a global community of unknown players through open text chat and real-time interaction.

🌐
Avakin Life Is a Persistent Online Social World Populated Entirely by Unknown Adults and Children Simultaneously
Unlike a game with defined objectives and structured play, Avakin Life is a social platform built around open-ended interaction in shared virtual spaces. The platform has no meaningful separation between adult and child users — anyone who downloads the app enters the same social world, inhabits the same virtual locations and can approach, chat with and form ongoing relationships with any other user present. The 3D avatar layer does not reduce the risk of these interactions; it simply makes the social environment feel safer and more familiar to children than it actually is.

Avakin Life is a virtual social world where users create customisable avatars and explore virtual environments with other players. Users can chat, socialise and participate in virtual activities with people from around the world. The platform focuses on social interaction inside a virtual world where users communicate through avatars — combining the immersive appeal of a game with the open, unstructured social dynamics of an adult social platform.
🧑‍🎤
Customisable Avatars in Shared 3D Social Spaces
Avakin Life allows users to build detailed, customisable avatars that move through persistent 3D virtual environments — apartments, clubs, beaches, social venues — populated in real time by other users. These shared spaces function as meeting places where conversations begin spontaneously between any users who happen to be present. A child's avatar is visible and approachable by any adult user in the same virtual location, with no restriction on who can initiate contact.
💬
Open Text Chat in Shared Virtual Environments
Within every shared virtual space in Avakin Life, users can send text messages to other avatars nearby — creating a public chat environment that resembles a social venue rather than a structured game. These conversations are unmoderated, happen in real time and can involve any combination of users present in the space. Children in these environments are receiving messages from unknown adults as a normal, expected part of the platform experience.
✉️
Private Direct Messaging Between Users
After meeting in a shared virtual space, any two users can continue their conversation through private direct messages entirely outside the virtual environment. These private chats are hidden from parents, carry no content filtering and create a sustained one-to-one communication channel between a child and any adult they have connected with inside the platform. The transition from public virtual chat to private messaging is a common pattern in online grooming.
💎
In-App Purchases for Avatar Items and Social Status
Avakin Life features an extensive in-app purchase system where real money is spent on avatar clothing, accessories, apartment furnishings and other status items. These purchases are directly tied to social standing within the virtual world — users with more elaborate avatars and spaces are perceived as having higher status. Children are exposed to sustained financial pressure to spend real money in order to participate fully in the platform's social culture and to feel accepted by their virtual peers.
🔓
No Effective Age Verification or User Separation
Avakin Life does not verify the age entered at registration and makes no attempt to separate adult users from child users within its virtual environments. A child who creates an account gains immediate access to the same shared virtual spaces, chat systems and social features used by adults — with no restriction, no moderated area and no indication to other users that they are interacting with a minor.
⚠️ Section 2

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from unrestricted stranger interaction in immersive virtual spaces, exposure to adult conversations, significant identity verification gaps and real financial pressure from in-app purchases.

👥
Stranger Interaction in Virtual Environments
Children share virtual spaces with unknown adults on equal footing — any adult present can approach a child's avatar and begin a conversation with no restriction, no screening and no parental awareness that contact has been made
🔞
Mature Conversations and Themes
Open chat in shared virtual spaces is unmoderated — children can be exposed to adult conversations, sexual themes, explicit language or other content that is entirely inappropriate for their age, delivered through the normalising context of avatar interaction
❓
Unverifiable User Identity and Age
There is no way for a child to know whether the user behind any avatar is another child or an adult — an avatar's appearance, name and stated age provide no reliable information about who is actually operating it
🔒
Privacy Risks From Personal Information
Children socialising in virtual environments may share real names, schools, ages or locations with users they feel they know through in-game interaction — without realising those users are unknown adults with no verified identity
😟
Grooming Risk via Private Messaging
The transition from public virtual chat to private direct messages is a well-documented grooming pathway — the immersive, relationship-building nature of virtual worlds makes children more susceptible to this progression than on conventional social media
💸
Financial Pressure From In-App Purchases
Avatar status is tied to real money spending — children face sustained social pressure to make in-app purchases to feel accepted, competitive or respected within the virtual community, often without understanding the real-world financial implications
🔴
Avakin Life Carries a High Risk Rating Unrestricted stranger interaction in immersive shared virtual spaces, unmoderated open chat, a private messaging system, no age verification or user separation and significant financial pressure from in-app purchases combine to place Avakin Life in the highest risk category for child safety on digital platforms.
🌐
Virtual Worlds Create a False Sense of Safety That Makes Children More Vulnerable, Not Less
The 3D avatar environment of Avakin Life creates a social experience that feels like play — but the interactions taking place within it are with real people, in real time, with no moderation and no parental visibility. Children who are appropriately cautious about speaking to strangers on conventional social media often engage freely and openly in virtual worlds because the context feels fictional or game-like. This misperception significantly increases their vulnerability, because the adults they are interacting with are real, their intentions are real, and the personal information children share is real and permanent.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Virtual Social Platforms Allow Children to Interact With Large, Unknown Online Communities
Avakin Life is not a game with a bounded player community — it is a persistent online social world with a large, open global user base of unknown individuals. When a child enters any shared virtual space on the platform, they are present in the same environment as adults from anywhere in the world, all of whom can approach, message and form relationships with them freely. The scale and openness of this community is not visible to a child navigating the platform, but it means the risk of encountering an adult with harmful intentions is persistent and ongoing throughout every session.
2
Avatar Communication Does Not Reduce the Risk of Real Harm
Because communication happens through avatars and text chat, children may not perceive virtual world interaction as carrying the same risks as direct video or phone contact with a stranger. This perception is incorrect and dangerous. The people operating every avatar in Avakin Life are real individuals — the conversations they have with a child are real, the relationships they build are real, and the personal information a child shares during those interactions is real. The avatar layer provides no protection and no barrier between a child and an adult with harmful intentions.
3
Some Virtual Spaces and Conversations May Not Be Appropriate for Younger Audiences
Avakin Life includes virtual venues — clubs, bars and social spaces — that are modelled on adult social environments. The conversations and themes that emerge in these virtual spaces reflect the adult user base that frequents them. A child who explores these areas of the platform is entering spaces that are not designed for or moderated to protect younger users — and may encounter explicit language, adult themes or other content long before any parent becomes aware it is happening.
4
In-App Purchase Pressure Begins Immediately and Is Deeply Embedded in the Social Experience
The financial mechanics of Avakin Life are not separate from its social experience — they are central to it. Avatar appearance, apartment quality and accessory collections are the primary signals of social status within the virtual world, and the pressure for children to spend money in order to keep up with peers or be taken seriously by other users begins from the first session. Parents should be aware that the app actively creates the conditions for repeated, ongoing spending and that children may not understand they are spending real money until significant amounts have already been committed.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around the specific risks of virtual social worlds, online stranger interaction and responsible digital behaviour.

🧑‍🎤
Talk With Children About Interacting Safely Online — Including in Virtual Worlds
Help your child understand that the rules of stranger safety apply in virtual worlds exactly as they do in any other online environment — and that an avatar is not a guarantee of who the real person behind it actually is. Be specific: explain that they should never share their real name, age, school, home town or any other identifying detail with someone they have only met inside a virtual world, regardless of how long they have been playing together or how friendly that person seems. Make clear that if any conversation in a virtual world makes them feel confused, uncomfortable or pressured, they should close the app immediately and speak to a parent — no explanation needed, no questions asked.
🛡️
Encourage Responsible Digital Behaviour and Privacy Awareness
Talk with your child about the specific ways virtual worlds differ from the games they might compare them to — the open social environment, the anonymous user base and the absence of any moderation or age verification. Help them understand that the in-app purchase pressure they feel is deliberately engineered, not a natural part of play, and that spending real money to improve their avatar's social status is not something they should do without explicit parental agreement. Children who understand the mechanics of platforms designed to maximise engagement and spending are significantly better equipped to navigate those pressures than those who encounter them without any prior preparation.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Manage Access to Virtual Social Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child has access to — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Virtual social world apps are frequently reinstalled after removal and are often introduced to children through peer recommendations from school friends who are already using them, making device-level controls far more effective than app-by-app management. Combine technical restrictions with consistent, honest conversations about why these platforms require careful thought at this stage of your child's development, to build the most complete and durable layer of protection available for your family.
