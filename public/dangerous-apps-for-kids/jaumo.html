👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Jaumo Is Designed for Adults 18 and Older
Jaumo's terms of service, feature set and global community are built for adult social and dating life. The platform offers no child-safe experience and does not apply content restrictions to the adult material visible throughout the app — including profiles, Stories and chat.
2
Global Reach Amplifies the Risk
Because Jaumo operates in over 180 countries and 40 languages, a child who creates a profile is not just interacting with local adults — they are accessible to an enormous international user base. This dramatically increases the range of unknown adults who can view their profile, send messages or initiate contact.
3
Curiosity and Peer Influence Are the Main Entry Points
Children may install Jaumo after encountering it through friends, social media or general curiosity about meeting new people online. These impulses are entirely natural — but the platform's scale, location features and unmoderated adult content make it particularly unsuitable for younger users.
4
Registration Is Easy to Complete With False Details
Jaumo requires only a date of birth and email address or social account to register. A child can create a full account in seconds by entering a false birthdate. No additional identity check stands between sign-up and complete access to the adult platform and its community.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting digital resilience.

💬
Discuss Online Relationships and Digital Safety With Children
Have calm, age-appropriate conversations about how global social apps work, why platforms like Jaumo are designed for adults, and what respectful, safe interaction online looks like. Help children understand that connecting with strangers across the world carries real risks — and that the app's friendly interface does not make those strangers safe or known.
🤝
Encourage Communication Within Trusted Social Circles
Help your child invest in the friendships and family bonds they already have, rather than seeking connection with unknown adults globally. Strong, trusted real-world relationships are the most powerful protective factor against the appeal of anonymous social discovery platforms.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Limit Access to Adult-Focused Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses. Check in-app purchase settings and review saved payment methods on shared devices regularly. Revisit your controls as your child grows — and keep the conversation going alongside the restrictions for the most complete protection.
