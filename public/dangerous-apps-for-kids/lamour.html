What This App Does

A clear picture of Lamour — a video chat and social discovery platform that connects users directly with strangers through one-to-one live video calls and text messaging, with no control over who children are matched or connected with.

📹
Lamour Is Designed to Connect Users With Strangers Through Instant Video Chat
Lamour's core purpose is to match users with people they have never met before and connect them immediately through live video calls and messaging. Unlike social discovery apps that require a separate step before contact can begin, Lamour places users directly into video conversations with unknown individuals as quickly as possible — removing every barrier between a child and a live, face-to-face interaction with an unknown adult anywhere in the world.

Lamour connects users directly into private, face-to-face live video calls with strangers they have never met before. Stranger Matching and Profile-Based Discovery
Lamour matches users with strangers based on profile browsing and discovery features, presenting a continuous feed of unknown individuals to interact with. Children are actively encouraged to browse, like and connect with people they have never met — with no verification of who those individuals are, what they want or how old they really are. The app's design frames stranger contact as the primary social activity.

Private Text Messaging With Connected Strangers
Once a connection is made through the app, Lamour allows users to continue conversations through private text messaging. These chats are entirely unmonitored, carry no content filtering and are completely invisible to parents — creating a persistent private communication channel between a child and any unknown adult they have connected with through the platform's matching features.
🎁
Virtual Gifts That Encourage Ongoing Contact
Lamour includes a virtual gift system that allows users to send digital gifts to people they are connected with, creating social incentives to maintain and develop ongoing contact with specific strangers. These mechanics are designed to build sustained engagement between matched users, creating pressure for children to remain in contact with adults they have only recently and randomly encountered through the app's discovery features.
🔓
No Effective Age Verification
Lamour does not verify the age provided at registration. A child gains full access to one-to-one video calling with strangers, private messaging and the gift-based social mechanics within moments of installing the app. Adults can equally enter any date of birth with nothing in place to verify or challenge the information they provide.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Video Chat Apps Designed for Meeting Strangers Carry the Highest Risk Profile for Children
Lamour is not a messaging platform for staying in touch with known contacts — it is specifically designed to connect users with people they have never met before through private, face-to-face video. This places it in a fundamentally different and significantly higher risk category than conventional social media, group video calling or messaging apps. Every conversation on Lamour is with a stranger, and the platform's design removes every barrier to that contact happening as quickly as possible.
2
Private Video Calls Leave No Record and Cannot Be Reviewed After the Fact
Unlike messaging apps where conversations can be reviewed if a concern arises, private video calls on Lamour leave no accessible log of what was said, shown or shared. A child can have a distressing or dangerous interaction, close the app and move on within seconds — with nothing recorded, no notification sent to a parent and no way of establishing what occurred. This makes any after-the-fact parental intervention practically impossible, and places all protective responsibility on the child during the call itself.
3
Children Are Not Equipped to Identify Manipulation in Real-Time Video Conversations
Adults who use these platforms to make contact with children typically do so gradually — presenting themselves as friendly, relatable or trustworthy before introducing inappropriate content or requests. Children are not well-equipped to identify these patterns in real-time, particularly in an intimate one-to-one video environment where social pressure to stay engaged is immediate and direct. The platform's gift mechanics further reinforce ongoing contact with specific individuals, compounding this risk over multiple interactions.
4
The App Is Easy to Install and Does Not Appear Explicitly Adult at First Glance
Lamour presents as a social and entertainment platform with an approachable, colourful interface — with no explicit adult content visible at the point of installation. A child may install the app out of curiosity or through peer recommendation without understanding that its core function is private video calling with unknown adults. Age is trivially easy to falsify at registration, removing the only nominal access control the platform applies before a child is fully inside the adult user environment.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting awareness around the specific risks of stranger video chat platforms and responsible digital behaviour.

🎓
Teach Children About the Risks of Video Chatting With Strangers
Help your child understand why private video calls with people they do not know are fundamentally unsafe — regardless of how friendly, relatable or trustworthy that person appears in conversation. Explain that anyone on a stranger-matching app can present themselves as a peer or friend while having very different intentions, and that an intimate, private video call creates an environment where inappropriate interactions can escalate quickly and without warning. Establish a clear household rule that private video calls with strangers are not an acceptable activity, and explain the reasoning — children who understand why respond to rules more consistently than those who simply receive them without explanation.
🤝
Encourage Responsible Digital Behaviour and Privacy Awareness
Talk regularly with your child about responsible online behaviour in the specific context of video and stranger-chat platforms — what personal information should never be shared with someone they have only just met through an app, how to recognise when a video conversation has moved in an uncomfortable direction, and exactly what to do when that happens. Children who are equipped with a clear understanding of privacy, identity risk and the limits of online trust are significantly more capable of protecting themselves independently in situations where you cannot intervene in real time.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Restrict Access to Stranger Video Chat Platforms
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child has access to — tablets, secondary phones and shared family devices included. Stranger video chat apps like Lamour are frequently reinstalled within minutes of being removed from a device, making device-level controls significantly more reliable than manual monitoring or app-by-app removal. Pair technical restrictions with consistent, open conversations about why these platforms are not appropriate, to build the most complete and durable layer of protection available for your family.
