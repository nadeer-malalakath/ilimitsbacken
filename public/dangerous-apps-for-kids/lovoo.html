Founded in Germany and widely used across Europe and beyond, LOVOO has tens of millions of registered users. Its location-based radar feature means children using the app are immediately visible to adult strangers in their immediate physical area — making geographic proximity both a core feature and a core risk.

Users discover matches, communicate through messaging and build social or romantic connections — all within an environment designed entirely for adult interaction.

Radar — Live Location Discovery
LOVOO's signature Radar feature displays other users on a real-time map ordered by physical proximity. A child using the app appears on this radar — visible to adult strangers who are nearby, sometimes within metres.

Swipe-Based Match Discovery
Users browse and swipe on profiles to express interest. Mutual likes open a private chat connection between adult strangers for the explicit purpose of social or romantic engagement.
🎥
Live Video Streaming
LOVOO features a live broadcast function where users stream video to the platform community and receive real-time comments and virtual gifts. Live streams are largely unmoderated and expose viewers to adult content in real time.

Unmoderated Private Messaging

💎
Credits System & Premium Tier
LOVOO uses a virtual credits currency and a premium subscription to unlock enhanced features. Credits can be purchased in-app using saved payment methods, creating a financial risk if a child accesses the platform.

LOVOO's credits system allows users to purchase premium interactions including profile boosts and live stream gifts. Children with access to a parent's device or stored payment method could purchase credits without parental awareness. Review payment settings on shared devices regularly.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Dating Apps Are Generally Intended for Adults 18 and Older
LOVOO's terms of service, community standards and entire feature set are built for adult social and romantic life. The platform makes no provision for younger users and does not filter adult content from the profiles, live streams and conversations visible throughout the app.
2
The Radar Is LOVOO's Most Significant Child Safety Risk
Unlike most dating apps that rely on profile swiping alone, LOVOO's real-time radar map actively broadcasts a child's physical location to nearby adult strangers the moment they open the app. This creates both a digital and a real-world risk that goes beyond typical online dating exposure.
3
Curiosity and Peer Influence Drive Installation
Children and teenagers may install LOVOO after seeing it among peers or encountering it through social media, particularly given its popularity across Europe. The app's friendly design and social features can make it feel like a harmless networking tool — masking its adult-only purpose and community.
4
Age Verification Is Straightforward to Bypass
LOVOO requires only a date of birth and email address or social account to register. A child can create a complete adult account in seconds by entering a false birthdate. No additional verification is required before the full platform — including the radar, live streams and private messaging — becomes accessible.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child and build lasting digital resilience.

💬
Discuss Online Relationships and Digital Safety With Children
Have open, calm conversations about how location-based apps like LOVOO work — particularly that the radar feature shares their real-time physical location with nearby adults. Help children understand that a friendly interface does not make unknown adults safe, and that digital interactions can have real-world consequences.
🤝
Encourage Communication Within Trusted Social Circles
Help your child invest in the friendships and family bonds they already have, rather than seeking connection with unknown adults through dating platforms. Strong, trusted relationships in the real world reduce the pull of anonymous social discovery apps and provide a healthier foundation for future connections.
🔒
Use Parental Controls to Limit Access to Adult-Focused Apps
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child uses. Review in-app purchase settings and saved payment methods on shared devices to prevent credit purchases. Revisit your controls regularly as your child grows — and keep honest family conversations running alongside them for the strongest protection.
