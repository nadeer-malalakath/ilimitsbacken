Dream11
Fantasy Sports & Betting Platform
🎰 Gambling / Betting ⚠️ High Risk for Children
Dream11 is a fantasy sports platform where users pay real money to enter contests and win cash prizes. While marketed as a skill game, it carries significant financial and psychological risks — especially for children and teenagers.
🚫
This App Is In Restricted Category Parents can choose to restrict apps like this as part of managing a child's digital environment.
📱

What This App Does

  • 🏆
    Fantasy Sports Platform
    Users build virtual cricket, football & kabaddi teams using real player stats.
  • 💸
    Requires Entry Fees
    Joining most contests requires depositing real money — from ₹10 to thousands.
  • 🎯
    Real Money Rewards
    Winners receive cash payouts. Losers lose their entry fee entirely.
  • 📢
    Promoted as a Skill Game
    Heavily advertised during IPL and major tournaments — normalising betting for kids.
  • 🎲
    Financial Risk Involved
    Outcomes depend on real match events; money can be lost quickly and repeatedly.
⚠️

Why It's Restricted for Children

👥

Ad Exposure

🧒

How It Can Affect Children

Impulsive Spending Behaviour
The thrill of winning encourages children to spend without thinking, often chasing losses.
Dopamine Reward Loops
Wins release dopamine, creating a cycle where children need bigger wins to feel the same thrill.
Reduced Academic Focus
Time spent on match analysis, team-building and contests takes away from studies and physical activity.
Normalisation of Gambling
Regular use makes betting feel ordinary and acceptable, shaping attitudes well into adulthood.
Financial Misunderstanding
Children may come to believe that money is won, not earned — undermining healthy financial values.
🔍

Warning Signs for Parents

  • ❓
    Frequently asking for money, pocket money top-ups or UPI access
  • ❓
    Secretive or protective phone usage — hiding screen or closing apps quickly
  • ❓
    Sudden strong interest in fantasy leagues, team stats or match betting
  • ❓
    Unexplained online transactions or app purchases on linked accounts
  • ❓
    Mood swings tied to sports outcomes — excited after wins, upset or withdrawn after losses

Tips for Parents

1
Talk openly about money and gambling
Explain the difference between skill, luck, and risk in age-appropriate terms.
2
Teach healthy financial habits early
Introduce saving, budgeting, and value of earnings through chores or projects.
3
Monitor app installations regularly
Periodically review installed apps and digital payment accounts on shared devices.
4
Set healthy digital boundaries together
Create screen time rules as a family — collaborative limits are easier to respect.
5
Encourage offline hobbies and activities
Sports, reading, arts and social activities reduce dependency on addictive apps.

🛡️ Why Parental Controls Help

Parental controls aren't about distrust — they're about protecting children from risks they don't yet fully understand. Restricting apps like Dream11 gives children the space to develop healthy relationships with technology, money, and entertainment before they encounter real financial stakes.

✅ Blocks gambling apps ✅ Limits screen time ✅ Filters adult content ✅ Builds safe habits
💚

Technology Can Be a Positive Force

When used responsibly and with guidance, technology helps children learn, create, and grow. Parental oversight isn't a barrier — it's a bridge that helps children build the digital wisdom they'll carry for life.
